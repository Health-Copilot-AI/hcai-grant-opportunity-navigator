## Mission Statement

> "The mission of the Jay L. Smith Family Foundation is to enhance the quality of life for individuals, families, and communities by funding qualified non-profit organizations that promote family values, support the advancement of healthcare, and serve the disadvantaged."

---

## Strategic Focus Areas

### Primary Priorities

The foundation supports organizations across several key areas, with particular emphasis on healthcare and elderly services:

#### 1. Alzheimer's Disease and Dementia Care (Highest Priority)
- The foundation is "especially partial to funding research for a cure of Alzheimer's"
- Dedicated to "assuring proper care and service for individuals suffering from this disease and related illnesses"
- Has funded leading Alzheimer's research facilities
- Recent grantee: Glenner Memory Care ($30,000 in 2024)

#### 2. Elderly Care
- Dedicated to funding organizations that provide vital services to the elderly
- Focus on hospitals and hospice facilities serving senior populations
- Addresses quality of life for aging individuals

#### 3. Cancer Research
- Supports cancer research and treatment organizations
- Recent grantee: Cancer Wellness Foundation of Central AL ($14,000 in 2024)
- Historically dedicated significant funds toward finding a cure for cancer

#### 4. Suicide Prevention
- Funds organizations researching suicide prevention methods
- Supports programs helping families coping with loss from suicide

#### 5. Domestic Violence Prevention
- Provides grants to domestic violence clinics
- Notable past grant to a domestic violence clinic in Montgomery, Alabama

### Secondary Focus Areas

- **Education**: Scholarships and educational institutions (e.g., Brigham Young University, Jones School of Law)
- **First Responders and Veterans**: Fallen Officers Fund of Douglas County ($30,000 in 2024)
- **Animal Welfare**: Animal rescue and welfare organizations
- **Food Security**: Community food programs
- **Human Services**: General community support services

### Eligibility Requirements

1. **501(c)(3) Status Required**: Must be recognized by IRS as tax-exempt charitable organization
2. **State/Local Recognition**: Must be recognized by state and local governments
3. **US-Based Only**: At this time, the foundation will only provide grants to entities and relief efforts in the United States
4. **One-Time Grants**: Should an organization be chosen for a grant, it shall only be a one-time grant
5. **Open to New Applicants**: Yes (12% new applicant funding rate)

## Application Process

### How to Apply

1. **Request Application**: Contact foundation through website (https://www.jaylsmithfamilyfoundation.org/request-an-application)
2. **Submit Required Documentation**: Complete application with 501(c)(3) verification
3. **Board Review**: Applications reviewed by Board of Directors

- Historically has funded 11+ organizations across 5+ states in first full year
- Demonstrates willingness to support diverse geographic areas

## NHELP Alignment Analysis

### Strong Alignment Areas

| Foundation Priority | NHELP Capability | Alignment Score |
|--------------------|------------------|-----------------|
| Elderly Care | Geriatric assessments, home visits to seniors, chronic disease management | HIGH |
| Healthcare Advancement | Community-based healthcare delivery, interprofessional education | HIGH |
| Serving the Disadvantaged | Underserved populations in Miami-Dade | HIGH |
| Family Values | Household-centered care model, multi-generational health | HIGH |
| Alzheimer's/Dementia | Cognitive screenings, care coordination for elderly | MODERATE |

### Potential Project Concepts

1. **Elder Health Initiative**: Enhanced geriatric care program for NHELP households with focus on:
   - Alzheimer's and dementia screening protocols
   - Fall prevention and home safety assessments
   - Chronic disease management for seniors
   - Care coordination with memory care specialists

2. **Senior Wellness and Prevention Program**:
   - Health education for aging-in-place
   - Social determinants screening for elderly isolation
   - Caregiver support and respite resources

3. **Suicide Prevention Integration**:
   - Mental health screening for elderly populations
   - Depression identification and referral pathways
   - Connection to behavioral health resources

### Grant Request Considerations

- **Recommended Ask**: $15,000 - $25,000 (mid-range of typical grants)
- **One-Time Funding**: Structure proposal as pilot or capacity-building
- **Measurable Outcomes**: Emphasize number of elderly served, screenings completed
- **Family Focus**: Highlight household-centered model aligning with "family values" mission
