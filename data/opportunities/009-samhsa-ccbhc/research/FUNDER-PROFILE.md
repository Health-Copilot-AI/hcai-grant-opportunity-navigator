## Mission & Priorities

### Mission Statement

> "Behavioral Health is Essential. Prevention Works. Treatment is Effective. People Recover."

*Source: https://www.samhsa.gov/about-us (Retrieved: 2026-02-03)*

### Agency Structure

SAMHSA operates within the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) and is organized into four primary centers:

1. **Center for Mental Health Services (CMHS)** - Mental health treatment, prevention, and recovery support
2. **Center for Substance Use Prevention (CSAP)** - Prevention programs and strategies
3. **Center for Substance Use Treatment (CSAT)** - Treatment services and recovery support
4. **Center for Behavioral Health Statistics and Quality (CBHSQ)** - Data collection, analysis, and program evaluation

Additionally, SAMHSA maintains **10 regional offices** across the United States to provide localized technical assistance and support.

*Source: https://www.samhsa.gov/about-us (Retrieved: 2026-02-03)*

### Strategic Priorities (FY 2026)

1. **CCBHC Expansion & Sustainability**: Expanding the Certified Community Behavioral Health Clinic model to serve more communities with comprehensive, integrated behavioral health services

2. **Crisis Services & 988 Lifeline**: Supporting 24/7 crisis intervention through the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline and community-based crisis services

3. **Substance Use Disorder Prevention & Treatment**: State Opioid Response (SOR) grants ($1.6 billion), Prevention, Treatment, and Recovery Services Block Grant ($2 billion)

4. **Mental Health Services**: Mental health activities ($2.8 billion), including children's mental health initiatives and serious mental illness programs

5. **Recovery Support Services**: Peer support, recovery community organizations, and recovery-oriented systems of care

6. **Behavioral Health Workforce Development**: Training, recruitment, and retention of behavioral health professionals

7. **Integration with Primary Care**: Supporting models that integrate behavioral health with physical health services

8. **Social Determinants of Health (SDOH)**: Addressing housing, employment, food security, and other social factors in behavioral health treatment

*Sources: https://www.samhsa.gov/about/budget (Retrieved: 2026-02-03); https://www.astho.org/advocacy/federal-government-affairs/leg-alerts/2026/summary-fy26-lhhs-bill-january-2026/ (Retrieved: 2026-02-03)*

### Target Populations

- Individuals with mental health disorders (serious mental illness, serious emotional disturbance)
- Individuals with substance use disorders (opioid use disorder, alcohol use disorder, stimulant use disorder)
- Veterans and active military personnel
- Children and adolescents with behavioral health needs
- Underserved and rural populations
- American Indian/Alaska Native communities
- Homeless individuals and families
- Justice-involved populations (re-entry services)
- People in recovery from mental health and substance use disorders

### Critical Requirements (CCBHC Expansion Grants)

#### CCBHC-PDI (Planning, Development, and Implementation) Track:
- [ ] **Eligible Organization Type**: Community-based behavioral health non-profit, local government behavioral health authority, or Indian Health Service/tribal organization
- [ ] **CCBHC Certification**: Must meet federal CCBHC certification criteria within 1 year of award
- [ ] **State Concurrence Letter**: Letter from state mental health authority approving proposal and indicating certification pathway
- [ ] **Geographic Catchment Area**: Defined service area without overlap with existing CCBHC grantees
- [ ] **Comprehensive Services Capability**: Ability to provide (directly or via partnership) all 9 required CCBHC services
- [ ] **24/7 Crisis Services**: Plan for establishing 24/7 crisis mental health services
- [ ] **Staffing Plan**: Part-time Project Director and Evaluator (requires SAMHSA approval)
- [ ] **Budget Compliance**: Maximum $1,000,000/year (direct + indirect costs)

#### CCBHC-IA (Improvement and Advancement) Track:
- [ ] **Existing CCBHC Status**: Must be state-certified CCBHC OR previous CCBHC Expansion grantee
- [ ] **Independent Compliance**: Must meet all CCBHC certification criteria independently (not as subcontractor)
- [ ] **No Geographic Overlap**: Cannot have overlapping catchment area with another CCBHC award
- [ ] **All CCBHC-PDI Requirements**: Same organizational, staffing, and budget requirements as PDI track

*Sources: https://www.samhsa.gov/grants/grant-announcements/sm-23-016 (Retrieved: 2026-02-03); https://www.samhsa.gov/grants/grant-announcements/sm-23-024 (Retrieved: 2026-02-03)*

## Decision Making

### Review Process

SAMHSA applications undergo a **multi-stage objective review process**:

1. **Eligibility Screening**: SAMHSA Division of Grant Review screens applications for responsiveness to NOFO requirements

2. **Technical/Merit Review**: Independent review panel evaluates applications based on published scoring criteria

3. **Second-Level Review**: SAMHSA National Advisory Council (when applicable) provides recommendations

4. **Funding Determination**: SAMHSA Administrator makes final funding decisions based on:
   - Objective review scores
   - Geographic distribution
   - Programmatic priorities
   - Availability of funds

*Source: https://www.samhsa.gov/grants/applying (Retrieved: 2026-02-03)*

### Review Criteria (CCBHC Expansion Grants)

Applications are typically scored on:

- **Project Narrative (65-75 points)**
  - Need for services and target population (15-20 points)
  - Proposed approach and service delivery model (25-30 points)
  - Staff and organizational capacity (15-20 points)
  - Data collection, performance measurement, and evaluation (10-15 points)

- **Budget and Budget Justification (15-20 points)**
  - Alignment of budget with proposed activities
  - Cost-effectiveness and sustainability plan

- **Additional Factors (10-15 points)**
  - Geographic distribution priority
  - Underserved populations focus
  - Health equity considerations

*Source: SAMHSA NOFO templates (Retrieved: 2026-02-03)*

### Decision Timeline

**6-8 months** from application deadline to award notification:
- Application deadline: Typically 60 days after NOFO release
- Review period: 60-90 days
- Approval process: 60-90 days
- Award notifications: Typically late September/early October

### Award Notification

Notifications are sent via **email to the Authorized Organizational Representative (AOR)** listed in the grants.gov application. Awards are also publicly listed on the SAMHSA Grants Dashboard.

*Source: https://www.samhsa.gov/grants/grants-dashboard (Retrieved: 2026-02-03)*

## Strategic Notes

1. **Partnership Model Recommended**: Given CCBHC certification requirements (24/7 crisis services, comprehensive behavioral health staff, substance use disorder treatment), FIU/NeighborhoodHELP should consider partnering with an existing Florida CCBHC (especially Community Health of South Florida in Miami-Dade) rather than pursuing independent CCBHC certification. Position NHELP as the **care coordination and SDOH infrastructure** supporting CCBHC clinical services.

2. **State Concurrence is Critical**: Any CCBHC application requires a formal letter from the Florida Department of Children and Families (state mental health authority) indicating concurrence and certification pathway. Early engagement with state officials is essential before applying.

3. **Track Selection Strategy**: CCBHC-PDI track is for NEW CCBHCs and requires achieving full certification within 1 year. CCBHC-IA track is only for organizations already certified or with prior CCBHC Expansion awards. FIU would need to pursue PDI track or partner with existing IA-eligible organization.

4. **FIU Thrive as Behavioral Health Platform**: The FIU Thrive digital platform could serve as the **care coordination technology backbone** for a CCBHC partnership, enabling integrated physical and behavioral health management, SDOH screening, and population health trackingâ€”all CCBHC requirements.

5. **Timing for FY 2026 Opportunity**: CCBHC-IA grants (SM-26-015) are forecasted to be released **March 31, 2026**. If FIU intends to pursue this opportunity, partnership discussions with Community Health of South Florida or another certified CCBHC should begin immediately (February/March 2026) to allow adequate time for partnership formalization, concept development, and state engagement before the 60-day application window.

6. **Workforce Development Angle**: FIU HWCOM's behavioral health training programs align with SAMHSA's workforce development priorities. Applications could emphasize training pipeline for behavioral health professionals, peer support specialists, and integrated care providers.

7. **Congressional Support Despite Administration Proposals**: The FY 2026 appropriations process demonstrated bipartisan Congressional commitment to SAMHSA programs, rejecting proposed cuts. This suggests program stability for multi-year grant planning.

8. **FQHC Eligibility Confirmed**: Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) ARE eligible for CCBHC grants if they meet all requirements. If FIU/NHELP operates as or partners with an FQHC, this pathway remains viable.
