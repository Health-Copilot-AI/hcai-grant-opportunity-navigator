## Letter of Inquiry Requirements

### LOI Format

- [ ] **Length**: 1-2 pages maximum
- [ ] **Format**: Professional business letter
- [ ] **Delivery**: U.S. Mail (not email for initial contact)

### LOI Content Checklist

- [ ] **Organization Introduction**
  - Brief description of NeighborhoodHELP/FIU HWCOM
  - Years of service in South Florida
  - Mission alignment with Peacock Foundation

- [ ] **Project Summary**
  - Specific project or program seeking funding
  - Clear, achievable goals within 1-2 year period
  - Why this is a catalytic investment opportunity

- [ ] **Community Impact**
  - Miami-Dade/South Florida focus
  - Populations served (underserved, uninsured)
  - Measurable outcomes expected

- [ ] **Funding Request**
  - Specific amount requested ($10K-$100K range)
  - How funds will be used
  - Other funding sources leveraged

- [ ] **Closing**
  - Request for opportunity to discuss further
  - Contact information
  - Offer for site visit

## Key Messaging Points

- **Catalyst Funding**: Frame as seed/expansion funding, not ongoing operational support

- **Local Impact**: Emphasize Miami-Dade County direct service and community presence

- **Measurable Outcomes**: Specific, achievable results within 1-2 year timeframe

- **Leveraged Investment**: Show how Peacock funding multiplies with other sources

- **Community Health Focus**: Primary care, preventive services, SDOH screening

- **Underserved Populations**: Uninsured, low-income residents of South Florida

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Email Submission** - LOI must be mailed, not emailed
2. **Excluded Project Types** - Don't request capital/construction funding
3. **Ongoing Support Framing** - Position as catalyst, not continuing support
4. **Vague Outcomes** - Be specific about measurable results
5. **Generic Content** - Tailor LOI specifically to Peacock priorities
6. **Missing Local Focus** - Emphasize Miami-Dade/South Florida impact
