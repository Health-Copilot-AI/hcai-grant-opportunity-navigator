## Our Story Page

### Founding
Peacock Foundation, Inc. was established in 1947 by Henry B. Peacock, Jr.

### Founder's Values
The Foundation's grantmaking reflects the enduring legacy of Henry B. Peacock, Jr., reflecting his beliefs in:
1. The importance of having strong values and moral character
2. Achieving self-sufficiency and, in turn, helping others
3. Being fiscally responsible stewards of philanthropic funds

### Impact Statement
> "Throughout its distinguished history, Peacock Foundation, Inc., has been a beacon of support, funding hundreds of non-profit organizations in their missions to improve the lives of many in Miami-Dade County."

### Organizational Philosophy
> "Peacock Foundation, Inc. prefers to maintain a low profile, as its grantee partners and people in need should be the center of attention."

### Transparency Commitment
The Foundation adheres to transparency and best practices, making financial information publicly available through CANDID's IRS Form 990-PF filings.

## Grant Application Process Page

### Eligibility
Applicants must be:
1. IRS recognized 501(c)(3) public charities classified as not a private foundation
2. Registered with the Department of Agriculture to solicit funds in Florida, when applicable
3. Located in and/or of significant benefit to residents primarily of Miami-Dade County

### Application Steps

#### Step 1: Letter of Inquiry (LOI)
Submit a brief letter via U.S. Mail containing:
- Organization mission
- Project description with intended outcomes
- Community served and grant period
- Funding amount requested and intended use

#### Step 2: Invitation to Apply
After staff reviews your LOI for eligibility and merit, you'll receive an email invitation with a specific deadline for full online proposal submission.

#### Step 3: Full Proposal Submission
Submit your complete proposal online by the designated deadline.

#### Step 4: Site Visit
Staff schedules an on-site visit to discuss your proposal further.

#### Step 5: Board Review
Complete proposals are presented to and reviewed by the Board four times a year.

#### Step 6: Decision Notification
Applicants are informed by email of the proposal's disposition immediately following the Board's decision.

#### Step 7: Grant Agreement
Organization leadership (Chair, President, or Executive Director) must electronically sign the agreement before payment is released.

### Key Notes
- **No LOI deadline** exists for initial inquiries
- Foundation staff cannot respond to inquiries by phone or email - U.S. Mail only
- No new proposals can be considered until all terms of Grant Agreement, narrative and financial reporting requirements are met

### Board Responsibilities
The Board provides direction to Peacock Foundation, Inc. and ensures best practices by:
- Defining grantmaking policies and guidelines
- Reviewing applications and determining grant awards at quarterly meetings
- Overseeing investments for organizational sustainability
- Participating in site visits and community engagement

### Governance Best Practices
- Formal orientation for new board members
- Written agreements regarding roles, responsibilities, and expectations
- Annual formal written assessment of the chief executive
- Conflict-of-interest policy with signed disclosure statements
- Inclusive board recruitment process for diversity of thought and leadership

### Historical Leadership - Barbara A. Rickard
> "Barbara A. Rickard began work with Mr. Peacock in November 1963 as a secretary and bookkeeper. In 1965, she became a Director of Peacock Foundation, Inc. Working tirelessly to help ensure the effectiveness of the Foundation's grantmaking, Ms. Rickard became President and CEO in the late 1990s. After nearly fifty years of service, she stepped down from the Board in January 2012. She passed away in April 2016."
