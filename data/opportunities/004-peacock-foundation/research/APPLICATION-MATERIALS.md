# Peacock Foundation Inc - Application Materials Guide

**Source:** https://peacockfoundationinc.org/grant-application-process/
**Retrieved:** 2026-02-02

---

## Application Overview

Peacock Foundation uses a **two-stage application process**:
1. Letter of Inquiry (LOI) - submitted by U.S. Mail
2. Full Proposal - submitted online (by invitation only)

**Key Distinction:** You cannot submit a full application without first receiving an invitation based on your LOI.

---

## Eligibility Requirements

### Organizational Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Tax Status | IRS recognized 501(c)(3) public charity |
| Classification | NOT a private foundation |
| State Registration | Registered with Florida Dept. of Agriculture (when applicable) |
| Geographic Scope | Located in and/or significantly benefiting Miami-Dade County residents |

### Ineligible Organizations
- Private foundations
- Organizations not registered in Florida (when required)
- Organizations primarily serving outside Miami-Dade County
- Organizations with unmet prior grant requirements

### Ineligible Request Types
- Capital campaigns
- Construction projects
- Renovation projects
- Requests to be primary/sole funder
- Endowment contributions

## Stage 1: Letter of Inquiry (LOI)

### Submission Method
**U.S. MAIL ONLY**

The foundation explicitly states staff cannot respond to inquiries by phone or email. Initial contact must be via postal mail.

### Mailing Address
```
Joelle Allen, Executive Director
Peacock Foundation, Inc.
4000 Ponce De Leon Blvd., Suite 450
Coral Gables, Florida 33146
```

### LOI Deadline
**None specified** - LOIs may be submitted at any time.

### Required LOI Content

Your letter should be brief and include:

1. **Organization Mission**
   - Clear statement of your organization's purpose
   - How long you have been operating
   - Who you serve

2. **Project Description with Intended Outcomes**
   - What you propose to do
   - Specific, measurable outcomes expected
   - How this aligns with Peacock's priorities (health, education, human services)

3. **Community Served and Grant Period**
   - Geographic focus within Miami-Dade County
   - Demographics of beneficiaries
   - Proposed timeline for the grant period

4. **Funding Amount Requested and Intended Use**
   - Specific dollar amount
   - How funds will be spent
   - Budget breakdown if space permits

### LOI Best Practices

**DO:**
- Keep it concise (1-2 pages recommended)
- Clearly demonstrate Miami-Dade County impact
- Align with stated funding priorities
- Include contact information
- Be specific about outcomes

**DON'T:**
- Submit via email or fax
- Include unsolicited attachments
- Request capital/construction funding
- Submit if you have outstanding grant requirements

## Stage 2: Invitation to Apply

### What Happens After LOI Submission

1. Staff reviews your LOI for:
   - Eligibility compliance
   - Alignment with funding priorities
   - Merit and community benefit

2. If approved, you receive:
   - Email invitation to submit full proposal
   - Specific deadline for submission
   - Instructions for online portal access

3. If not approved:
   - Notification of decision (method not specified)

### Timeline Considerations
- Board meets quarterly (4 times per year)
- Allow adequate time for LOI review before next board meeting
- Full proposal deadline will be set by foundation

---

## Stage 3: Full Proposal Submission

### Submission Method
**ONLINE** (portal access provided with invitation)

### Likely Required Components

Based on typical foundation requirements and available information:

#### Organizational Information
- Legal name and EIN
- Contact information
- Brief organizational history
- Current programs and services
- Annual operating budget
- Current year financials

#### Project Details
- Detailed project description
- Goals and objectives
- Implementation timeline
- Target population and numbers served
- Staff involved
- Partnerships/collaborations

#### Outcomes and Evaluation
- Specific measurable outcomes
- Methods of measurement
- Reporting plan
- Sustainability after grant period

#### Budget
- Detailed project budget
- Other funding sources (secured and pending)
- In-kind contributions
- Administrative/overhead costs

#### Attachments (Likely Required)
- 501(c)(3) determination letter
- Board list with affiliations
- Most recent audited financial statements
- Current year budget
- Key staff resumes (if relevant)

---

## Stage 4: Site Visit

### What to Expect
After full proposal submission, foundation staff will schedule an on-site visit.

### Purpose
- Verify organizational capacity
- Discuss proposal in detail
- Meet key staff
- Observe programs if applicable
- Answer questions from foundation

### Preparation Tips
1. Have key staff available
2. Prepare program demonstration if relevant
3. Have financials accessible
4. Be ready to discuss:
   - How Peacock funding specifically helps
   - Measurable outcomes
   - Organizational sustainability
   - Community partnerships

---

## Stage 5: Board Review

### Review Schedule
Board meets **four times per year** (quarterly).

### Review Process
- Complete proposals presented to full Board
- Board deliberates and makes decisions
- Decisions made during meeting

### Decision Factors (Inferred)
Based on foundation priorities:
- Alignment with health/education/human services focus
- Miami-Dade County community benefit
- Organizational capacity
- Measurable outcomes
- Reasonable budget
- Fiscal responsibility
- Sustainability

---

## Stage 6: Decision Notification

### Notification Method
**Email** - sent immediately following Board decision

### Possible Outcomes
1. **Approved** - proceed to grant agreement
2. **Declined** - may or may not include feedback
3. **Deferred** - additional information requested

---

## Stage 7: Grant Agreement

### Signing Authority
Must be signed by organization leadership:
- Board Chair, OR
- President, OR
- Executive Director

### Signing Method
**Electronic signature**

### Important Notes
- Payment NOT released until agreement signed
- Agreement includes reporting requirements
- All terms must be met before future proposals

---

## Post-Award Requirements

### Grant Reports
All grantees must submit end-of-grant reports including:

1. **Narrative Report**
   - Activities completed
   - Outcomes achieved
   - Challenges encountered
   - Lessons learned

2. **Financial Report**
   - How grant funds were spent
   - Comparison to proposed budget
   - Documentation of expenditures

### Compliance
> "No new proposals can be considered until all terms of Grant Agreement, narrative and financial reporting requirements are met."

## Strategic Recommendations

### For Maximum Success

1. **Align Clearly with Priorities**
   - Health: Primary care, mental health, oral health
   - Education: With self-sufficiency foundation
   - Human Services: Youth, elderly, vulnerable populations

2. **Emphasize Miami-Dade Impact**
   - Be specific about geographic area
   - Quantify beneficiaries
   - Demonstrate community need

3. **Request Reasonable Amounts**
   - Median grant: $25,000
   - Average grant: $32,000
   - Sweet spot: $15,000-$50,000

4. **Demonstrate Fiscal Responsibility**
   - Aligns with founder's values
   - Show multiple funding sources
   - Present realistic budget

5. **Plan for Relationship Building**
   - Foundation values trust-based relationships
   - Long-term partnerships preferred
   - Be prepared for multi-year engagement
