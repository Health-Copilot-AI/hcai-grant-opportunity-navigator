## About / History Page

**Source:** https://miamifoundation.org/who-we-are/our-history/

### Founding Story
On January 26, 1967, 21 great Miamians, including newspaper magnate James L. Knight and media pioneer Mitchell Wolfson, brought together thousands of dollars to create a "living philanthropy" tasked with improving life in Greater Miami forever.

### Historical Identity
Originally established as the Dade Community Foundation. The organization serves as Greater Miami's permanent philanthropic endowment.

### Impact Metrics
- Total invested into community: $905+ million
- Assets under management: ~$427 million (as of recent reporting)
- Grants awarded to date: $505+ million
- Fundholders: 1,000+
- Total donors: 60,000+

### 50th Anniversary (2017)
- Year-long celebration
- $1 million in strategic grant investments across four focus areas: Legacy, Opportunity, Resiliency, and Creativity
- Launched Miami Forever Fund with $5 million fundraising goal

### Major Crisis Response
- **COVID-19 (2020):** Mobilized $16+ million supporting 600 nonprofits
- **Surfside Building Collapse (2021):** Co-established Support Surfside fund raising ~$5 million
- **Hurricanes Irma and Maria (2017):** Raised $6.4 million for recovery
- **Census 2020:** Miami-Dade Counts advocacy campaign
- **Thrive 305:** Civic engagement initiative promoting inclusive government participation

### Core Activities
The Foundation connects people to knowledge, mobilizes communities toward action, and facilitates collaborative solutions.

## Community Grants Program Page

**Source:** https://miamifoundation.org/community-grants/

### Program Overview
Community Grants invests in an organization's mission with two years of general operating support. As a permanent resource for Greater Miami.

### Current Status
The 2025-27 Community Grants application cycle has closed. Community Grants will open applications again in 2027.

### Investment
$7 million over two years across seven funding priorities (in partnership with The Frederick A. DeLuca Foundation, Miami-Dade County, the State Attorney's Office of Miami-Dade County and the Taft Foundation).

### Award Amounts
Grant sizes are dependent on organizational annual operating budget, ranging from $40,000 to $70,000 for two years, distributed as mission-building, general operating support.

### Seven Funding Priorities

1. **Access to Health and Wellness**
   - Improve health outcomes
   - Connect to quality health resources and education
   - Reduce health disparities
   - Address social determinants of health

2. **Economic Stability**
   - Meet local affordability challenges
   - Promote self-sufficiency
   - Support entrepreneurship
   - Enable financial independence and personal stability

3. **Inclusion of People with Disabilities**
   - Expand opportunities for people with disabilities

4. **Inspiring Arts and Culture**
   - Strengthen communities through arts
   - Cultural initiatives

5. **Safer Communities**
   - Crime prevention and deterrence
   - Community safety activation
   - Economic and educational mobility
   - Support for survivors

6. **Sustainable Environment**
   - Environmental education
   - Climate resilience
   - Biscayne Bay restoration
   - Everglades preservation
   - Extreme heat mitigation

7. **Thriving Youth and Families**
   - Education and development
   - All age groups

### Application Review Process
- Community Advisors program
- 100+ community advisors review proposals
- Diverse personal and professional experiences
- 48+ hours of meetings
- Trust-based philanthropy approach

### Moratorium Policy
Organizations that receive grants in both the 2023 and 2025 cycles will face a moratorium in 2027.

### Program Contact
- Community Engagement Manager: Julissa Rivera
- Email: jrivera@miamifoundation.org
