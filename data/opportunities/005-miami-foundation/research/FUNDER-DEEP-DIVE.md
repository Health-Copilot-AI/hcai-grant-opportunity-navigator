## Executive Summary

The Miami Foundation is Greater Miami's community foundation, established in 1967 (originally as the Dade Community Foundation). With over $565 million in total assets (2024) and having invested more than $905 million in the community since inception, it represents the largest and most influential philanthropic institution in Miami-Dade County. The Foundation operates a robust Community Grants program with bi-annual cycles, investing $7 million per cycle across seven funding priorities.

## Grant Programs Portfolio

### 1. Community Grants (Flagship Program)
- **Investment:** $7 million over two years (2025-2027 cycle)
- **Awards:** $40,000 - $70,000 per organization (based on operating budget)
- **Funding Type:** General operating support (unrestricted)
- **Duration:** Two years
- **Cycle:** Bi-annual (next opens 2027)
- **2025 Grantees:** 127 organizations

### 2. Safe Communities Program
- **Partner:** Miami-Dade State Attorney's Office
- **Focus:** Crime prevention, survivor services, youth development
- **Status:** Integrated into Community Grants as funding priority (2025)

### 3. Perez CreARTE Grants
- **Partner:** The Jorge M. Perez Family Foundation
- **Investment:** $5.05 million (2025)
- **Cumulative (since 2019):** $11+ million to 47 organizations
- **Reach:** 700,000+ individuals served
- **Focus:** Arts access, arts education, artist fellowships/residencies

### 4. Arts Access Catalyst Grant
- **Award:** Up to $20,000 over two years
- **Focus:** Arts partnerships with Miami-Dade County Public Schools
- **Partners:** 12 inaugural grantees

### 5. Miami Fellows Program
- **Duration:** 15 months
- **Class XII:** 18 fellows (2025)
- **Focus:** Leadership development for emerging civic leaders
- **Eligibility:** 5-15 years into professional career
- **Funders:** W.K. Kellogg Foundation, John S. and James L. Knight Foundation

### 6. Other Programs
- **Miami Open for Business** - Asset purchases for underserved nonprofits/small businesses
- **Miami Connected** - Digital equity programming
- **Miami Disaster Resilience Fund** - Disaster preparedness and recovery
- **LGBTQ+ Equity Fund** - Partnership with National LGBTQ Task Force
- **Racial Equity Fund** - Support for Black-led organizations
- **Sankofa Sistas Giving Circle** - Black women and girls empowerment

## Community Grants - Detailed Analysis

### Seven Funding Priorities (2025-2027)

1. **Access to Health and Wellness**
   - Improve health outcomes
   - Connect residents to quality health resources and education
   - Reduce health disparities
   - Address social determinants of health

2. **Economic Stability**
   - Address local affordability challenges
   - Promote self-sufficiency and entrepreneurship
   - Support financial independence and personal stability

3. **Inclusion of People with Disabilities**
   - Expand opportunities for people with disabilities

4. **Inspiring Arts and Culture**
   - Strengthen community through arts and cultural initiatives

5. **Safer Communities**
   - Crime prevention and deterrence
   - Substance abuse reduction
   - Youth safety programs
   - Support for survivors of trafficking, gun violence, and crime

6. **Sustainable Environment**
   - Environmental education
   - Climate resilience
   - Biscayne Bay restoration
   - Everglades preservation
   - Extreme heat mitigation

7. **Thriving Youth and Families**
   - Education and development opportunities across all age groups

### Selection Criteria

The Foundation prioritizes organizations that:
1. **Advance opportunities for marginalized individuals and communities**
2. **Focus on progress** with clear goals for growth
3. **Build long-lasting solutions** for individuals and communities
4. **Collaborate with community** with representative, inclusive leadership
5. **Demonstrate sustainability** with high-quality, viable work

### Community Advisors Program
- 100+ community advisors review proposals
- Diverse personal and professional experiences
- 48+ hours of review meetings
- Trust-based philanthropy approach

### Funding Partners (2025 Cycle)
- The Frederick A. DeLuca Foundation
- Miami-Dade County
- State Attorney's Office of Miami-Dade County
- The Taft Foundation

## History & Milestones

### Founding (1967)
- January 26, 1967: 21 Miami leaders including James L. Knight and Mitchell Wolfson established the Dade Community Foundation
- Purpose: Create a "living philanthropy" to improve Greater Miami forever

### Leadership Evolution
- **Ruth Shack** - Founding President (1985-2009)
- **Rebecca Fishman Lipsey** - President/CEO (2020-present)

### Major Milestones
- **1967:** Founded as Dade Community Foundation
- **1992:** Received 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status
- **1999:** Miami Fellows program launched
- **2017:** 50th Anniversary celebration; launched Miami Forever Fund ($5M goal)
- **2017:** $6.4 million raised for Hurricanes Irma and Maria recovery
- **2020:** Mobilized $16+ million for COVID-19 response (600 nonprofits supported)
- **2020:** Census advocacy campaign (Miami-Dade Counts 2020)
- **2021:** Support Surfside fund (~$5 million for building collapse)
- **2019-2025:** Perez CreARTE program invested $11+ million
