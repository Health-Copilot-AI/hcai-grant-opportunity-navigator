## Award Structure

### Grant Amounts
Awards based on organizational annual operating budget:

| Operating Budget | Award Amount (2 Years) |
|------------------|------------------------|
| Smaller organizations | $40,000 |
| Mid-size organizations | $50,000 - $60,000 |
| Larger organizations | Up to $70,000 |

### Funding Type
- **General Operating Support** (unrestricted)
- "Mission-building" support
- Organization discretion on use
- NOT project-specific funding

### Grant Period
- Duration: Two years (24 months)
- Example: July 1, 2025 - June 30, 2027

## Eligibility Requirements

### Organizational Eligibility

**Required:**
- [ ] Active 501(c)(3) status as a public charity
- [ ] OR established fiscal sponsor with 501(c)(3) status
- [ ] OR government entity (nonprofit equivalent)
- [ ] OR religious organization (nonprofit equivalent)
- [ ] Work directly serves Miami-Dade County residents
- [ ] Compliance with any existing Foundation grants

**Geographic:**
- Must serve Miami-Dade County
- Primary impact in Greater Miami

### Moratorium Policy
- Organizations receiving grants in both 2023 and 2025 cycles face moratorium in 2027
- Cannot apply in consecutive cycles if awarded twice in a row

### Disqualifying Factors
- Not tax-exempt or without proper fiscal sponsorship
- Work does not serve Miami-Dade residents
- Outstanding compliance issues with Foundation
- Subject to moratorium from consecutive awards

## Seven Funding Priorities

Applicants must align with ONE primary funding priority:

### 1. Access to Health and Wellness
**Focus Areas:**
- Improve health outcomes
- Connect residents to quality health resources
- Health education
- Reduce health disparities
- Address social determinants of health

**Relevant for:** Community health centers, health education nonprofits, SDOH-focused organizations

### 2. Economic Stability
**Focus Areas:**
- Meet local affordability challenges
- Self-sufficiency programs
- Entrepreneurship support
- Financial independence
- Personal stability

**Relevant for:** Workforce development, financial literacy, housing support, small business assistance

### 3. Inclusion of People with Disabilities
**Focus Areas:**
- Expand opportunities
- Accessibility improvements
- Employment and education access
- Community integration

**Relevant for:** Disability services, accessibility advocates, inclusive programming

### 4. Inspiring Arts and Culture
**Focus Areas:**
- Strengthen communities through arts
- Cultural initiatives
- Arts access
- Creative expression

**Relevant for:** Arts organizations, cultural institutions, creative education

### 5. Safer Communities
**Focus Areas:**
- Crime prevention and deterrence
- Community safety activation
- Economic and educational mobility
- Substance abuse services
- Support for survivors (trafficking, gun violence, crime)

**Relevant for:** Violence prevention, survivor services, reentry programs, youth safety

### 6. Sustainable Environment
**Focus Areas:**
- Environmental education
- Climate resilience
- Biscayne Bay restoration
- Everglades preservation
- Extreme heat mitigation

**Relevant for:** Environmental organizations, climate advocacy, conservation groups

### 7. Thriving Youth and Families
**Focus Areas:**
- Education opportunities
- Development programs
- All age groups
- Family support

**Relevant for:** Youth development, education nonprofits, family services

## Selection Criteria

### Five Core Criteria

The Foundation prioritizes organizations that demonstrate:

#### 1. Advance Opportunities for Marginalized Communities
- Work directly with marginalized communities
- Address disparities around:
  - Race
  - Age
  - Ethnicity
  - Gender identity
  - Sexual orientation
  - Mixed-abilities
  - Other lines of difference

#### 2. Focus on Progress
- Clear goals for growth
- Timely impact potential
- Meaningful opportunities for advancement
- Measurable outcomes orientation

#### 3. Build Lasting Solutions
- Long-term change focus
- Sustainable impact models
- Systems-level thinking
- Community transformation

#### 4. Collaborate with Community
- Leadership representative of served communities
- Inclusive organizational culture
- Community feedback mechanisms
- Participatory approaches

#### 5. Organizational Sustainability
- High-quality, viable work
- Strong organizational health
- Financial stability
- Capacity to utilize and steward funding

## Review Process

### Community Advisors Program

**Structure:**
- 100+ community advisors review proposals
- Diverse personal and professional backgrounds
- Experience related to funding categories
- Volunteer community members

**Process:**
- 48+ hours of review meetings
- Multi-step evaluation
- Consensus-based recommendations
- Trust-based philanthropy approach

**Note:** Applicant organizations may participate as Community Advisors in categories OTHER than their application category.

### Review Timeline
1. **Application Submission** - via Submittable
2. **Initial Screening** - eligibility verification
3. **Community Advisor Review** - scoring and discussion
4. **Staff Review** - due diligence
5. **Final Selection** - board approval
6. **Notification** - successful and unsuccessful applicants informed

## Application Tips

### Do:
- **Align clearly** with ONE funding priority
- **Demonstrate** community voice and representation
- **Show** organizational sustainability
- **Quantify** impact where possible
- **Be specific** about Miami-Dade service
- **Highlight** marginalized community focus
- **Describe** collaborative approaches

### Don't:
- Apply to multiple funding priorities
- Focus on project-specific requests (this is general operating support)
- Ignore the five selection criteria
- Overstate reach or impact
- Submit incomplete applications
- Miss the deadline

### Strong Applications:
- Clear mission-priority alignment
- Evidence of community engagement
- Diverse, representative leadership
- Sustainable organizational model
- Measurable impact demonstrated
- Financial health indicators
- History of effective programs

## Positioning for NeighborhoodHELP/FIU Thrive

### Primary Funding Priority
**Access to Health and Wellness**

### Key Alignment Points
1. Quality health resources through mobile delivery
2. Health education embedded in service model
3. Reducing health disparities for underserved communities
4. SDOH screening and navigation
5. Miami-Dade County direct service

### Secondary Alignment (if applicable)
- **Economic Stability:** Employment/training components
- **Thriving Youth and Families:** Family health services

### Positioning Framework

**Mission Alignment:**
"NeighborhoodHELP/FIU Thrive expands access to health and wellness services for Miami-Dade's underserved residents, addressing health disparities and social determinants of health through community-embedded delivery models."

**Community Connection:**
- Service in underserved Miami-Dade neighborhoods
- Partnerships with community organizations
- Community health worker model
- Responsive to community-identified needs

**Organizational Sustainability:**
- FIU institutional backing
- Diversified funding streams
- Established track record
- Professional leadership

- Patient/client numbers served
- Health outcomes improved

- Community partnerships
