## Mission and Vision

### Mission Statement

> "Bringing together thousands of changemakers, philanthropists, nonprofit, government, and corporate leaders to solve community challenges together."

### Vision

> "Together we are building a Greater Miami where everyone can thrive."

### Core Values

The Foundation's work is guided by:
1. **Equity & Inclusion** - Prioritizing marginalized communities
2. **Collaboration** - Cross-sector partnerships
3. **Trust-Based Philanthropy** - Flexible, unrestricted funding
4. **Community Voice** - Community advisors in grantmaking decisions
5. **Long-Term Impact** - Permanent resource for Greater Miami

### Historical Context

- Established in 1967 as "living philanthropy" to improve Greater Miami forever
- Over 55+ years of continuous grantmaking
- Invested $905+ million in the community since inception
- First 50 years (1967-2017): $485 million distributed
- Serves 1,000+ fundholders and 60,000+ donors

## Funding Priorities

### Community Priority Areas

The Foundation operates across five core functions:
1. **Develop Leaders** - Cultivating emerging changemakers (Miami Fellows program)
2. **Collaborate on Solutions** - Facilitating cross-sector partnerships
3. **Accelerate Generosity** - Mobilizing philanthropic resources
4. **Exchange Knowledge** - Sharing information and best practices
5. **Support Nonprofits** - Resources and grants to community organizations

### Community Grants Program - Seven Funding Priorities (2025-2027)

#### 1. Access to Health and Wellness â­ PRIMARY FIT FOR FIU
- Improve health outcomes
- Connect residents to quality health resources and education
- Reduce health disparities
- Address social determinants of health

#### 2. Economic Stability
- Address local affordability challenges
- Promote self-sufficiency and entrepreneurship
- Support financial independence and personal stability

#### 3. Inclusion of People with Disabilities
- Expand opportunities for people with disabilities

#### 4. Inspiring Arts and Culture
- Strengthen community through arts and cultural initiatives

#### 5. Safer Communities
- Crime prevention and deterrence
- Substance abuse reduction
- Youth safety programs
- Support for survivors of trafficking, gun violence, and crime

#### 6. Sustainable Environment
- Environmental education
- Climate resilience
- Biscayne Bay restoration
- Everglades preservation
- Extreme heat mitigation

#### 7. Thriving Youth and Families
- Education and development opportunities across all age groups

### Selection Criteria

The Foundation prioritizes organizations that:
1. **Advance opportunities for marginalized individuals and communities**
2. **Focus on progress** with clear goals for growth
3. **Build long-lasting solutions** for individuals and communities
4. **Collaborate with community** with representative, inclusive leadership
5. **Demonstrate sustainability** with high-quality, viable work

### Geographic Focus

| Priority | Geographic Scope |
|----------|------------------|
| **Primary** | Greater Miami-Dade County, Florida (95%+ of grants) |
| **Requirement** | Organizations must directly serve Miami-Dade County residents |

## Application Process

### Community Grants Program Timeline

| Phase | Timing | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **Application Cycle Opens** | Every 2 years | Next cycle: 2027 |
| **2025 Cycle** | CLOSED (Feb 28, 2025) | 2027 preparation recommended |
| **Application Platform** | Submittable | https://themiamifoundation.submittable.com |
| **Grant Period** | 2 years | $40K-$70K based on operating budget |

### Eligibility Requirements

1. IRS-recognized 501(c)(3) public charity (or public university like FIU)
2. Work directly serves Miami-Dade County residents
3. Alignment with at least one of seven funding priorities
4. Strong organizational sustainability
5. Clear community impact metrics

### Application Steps

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **1. Monitor for Cycle Opening** | Subscribe to newsletter for 2027 cycle announcement |
| **2. Online Application** | Submit via Submittable portal when cycle opens |
| **3. Community Advisor Review** | 100+ advisors review proposals (48+ hours of meetings) |
| **4. Staff Review** | Foundation staff conducts due diligence |
| **5. Board Decision** | Final approval by Board of Trustees |
| **6. Grant Agreement** | Electronic agreement signed before disbursement |
| **7. Two-Year Grant Period** | Unrestricted operating support with reporting requirements |

### Key Process Notes

- **No LOI required** - Direct to full application when cycle opens
- **Community voice central** - Community advisors play significant role
- **Trust-based approach** - Unrestricted funding, flexible reporting
- **Moratorium policy** - Organizations cannot apply in consecutive cycles
- **Highly competitive** - 900+ applications for ~127 awards (14% success rate)

## Major Milestones and History

### Founding and Early Years
- **1967:** Founded as Dade Community Foundation by 21 Miami leaders
- **1985-2009:** Ruth Shack served as Founding President
- **1992:** Received 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status
- **1999:** Miami Fellows program launched

### Recent Achievements
- **2017:** 50th Anniversary celebration; launched Miami Forever Fund ($5M goal)
- **2017:** $6.4 million raised for Hurricanes Irma and Maria recovery
- **2019-2025:** Perez CreARTE program invested $11+ million in arts access
- **2020:** Mobilized $16+ million for COVID-19 response (600 nonprofits supported)
- **2020:** Census advocacy campaign (Miami-Dade Counts 2020)
- **2020:** Rebecca Fishman Lipsey became President & CEO
- **2021:** Support Surfside fund (~$5 million for building collapse recovery)
- **2024:** Assets reached $565 million (50%+ growth since 2020)
