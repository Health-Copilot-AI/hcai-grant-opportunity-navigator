## STRATEGIC NOTES

### Why This Approach?

1. **Respects Their Model**: GreenLight Fund's selection process is intentional and research-based. Unsolicited proposals are counterproductive.

2. **Creates Real Value**: By positioning FIU as a resource to their grantees, you become useful rather than demanding.

3. **Opens Multiple Doors**: Even if FIU isn't a direct grantee, partnership with GreenLight orgs could lead to project funding and valuable collaborations.

4. **Long-term Relationship**: Building a genuine partnership now positions FIU well if GreenLight expands or creates new funding vehicles in the future.

5. **Demonstrates Understanding**: Showing that you understand their invitation-only model and respect their process builds credibility.

### Success Metrics (Not Grant Success, But Engagement Success)

- [ ] Established communication with Valeria Perez-Ferreiro
- [ ] GreenLight leadership understands FIU's Thrive platform and relevant capabilities
- [ ] At least one partnership discussion initiated with a GreenLight-funded organization
- [ ] FIU positioned as a trusted community resource for GreenLight's work
- [ ] Quarterly relationship maintenance established

## COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

- **DON'T**: Send unsolicited grant proposals
- **DON'T**: Try to "pitch" Thrive as a product to sell
- **DON'T**: Assume FIU is in their target pool without confirmation
- **DON'T**: Use boilerplate grant language or formal proposal tone in initial outreach
- **DON'T**: Make the conversation about FIU's funding needs; focus on how FIU can help their work
- **DON'T**: Ignore their invitation-only model or try to "game" their selection process
- **DON'T**: Contact multiple GreenLight staff simultaneously; work through primary contact
