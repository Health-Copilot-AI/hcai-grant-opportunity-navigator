## Mission and Purpose

### Mission Statement

NACHC represents Community Health Centers (CHCs) and advocates for policies that support access to quality, affordable healthcare for underserved populations. The organization serves as the primary voice for health centers at the national level.

### Role in Healthcare

NACHC is the national membership organization for Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) and Look-Alike health centers. It provides:

- **Advocacy**: Federal and state policy representation
- **Education**: Training and professional development
- **Resources**: Technical assistance and best practices
- **Partnerships**: Connecting health centers with funders and partners
- **Grantmaking**: Administering mobile health and innovation grants

## Grant Programs

### Important Note on Grantmaking

NACHC is primarily a **membership organization and advocacy group**, not a traditional grantmaking foundation. Grant programs are typically funded by external partners (Leon Lowenstein Foundation, Direct Relief) and administered by NACHC.

### Mobile Health Advancement Awards (Primary Grant Program)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Program Name** | Mobile Health Advancement: Operations, Outreach, Expansion |
| **Award Amount** | $20,000 per award |
| **Number of Awards** | 10 |
| **Total Program Funding** | $200,000 |
| **Funders** | Leon Lowenstein Foundation, Direct Relief |
| **Administrator** | NACHC |

#### Funding Tracks

1. **Staff Training** - Workforce capacity building for mobile health operations
2. **Outreach Activities** - Community engagement and patient outreach
3. **Technology/Operations Expansion** - Equipment, technology, or operational enhancements

#### Eligibility Requirements

- Must be a Section 330 funded FQHC or Look-Alike health center organization
- Must have active NACHC Organizational Health Center Membership in good standing
- Membership must have been active as of March 31 of application year
