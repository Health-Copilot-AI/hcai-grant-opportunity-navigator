## Mission and Strategic Priorities

The Bureau of Primary Health Care (BPHC) supports the Health Center Program, which improves the health of the nation's underserved communities and vulnerable populations by assuring access to comprehensive, culturally-competent, quality primary health care services.

### Health Center Program Scope

- **Current Reach**: Over 1,400 HRSA-funded health centers
- **Service Sites**: Approximately 14,000 service delivery sites
- **Patients Served**: Over 30 million patients annually
- **Coverage**: All 50 states, DC, Puerto Rico, Virgin Islands, and the Pacific Basin

## Key Funding Programs

### 1. New Access Points (NAP)

**NOFO Number**: HRSA-25-085
**Purpose**: Expand access to affordable, accessible, quality primary health care by establishing new service delivery sites

#### FY 2025 NAP Details

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Total Funding | $50 million expected |
| Number of Awards | Approximately 77 awards |
| Period of Performance | June 1, 2025 - May 31, 2026 (1 year) |
| Grants.gov Number | 349046 |

#### Eligibility Requirements

**Eligible Organizations**:
- Currently funded health centers (H80 award recipients)
- Health Center Program Look-Alikes
- New organizations meeting Health Center Program requirements
- Public or nonprofit private entities
- Tribal organizations
- Faith-based organizations
- Community-based organizations

**Site Requirements**:
- Must propose at least one new full-time, permanent access point
- Primary medical care must be the site's main purpose
- Urban sites: Within 15 miles of other organization sites
- Rural sites: Within 30 miles of other organization sites

#### Sub-Programs (Population Types)

| Sub-Program | Abbreviation | Target Population |
|-------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Community Health Center | CHC | General underserved |
| Migrant Health Center | MHC | Migratory/seasonal agricultural workers |
| Health Care for the Homeless | HCH | Homeless individuals/families |
| Public Housing Primary Care | PHPC | Public housing residents |

**Note**: Applicants may request funding for one or more population types

#### Key Requirements

- Must provide health services within 120 days of award
- Must maintain H80 award status to retain NAP funding
- Must comply with Health Center Program requirements
- Subject to geographic limitations relative to existing sites

|-----------|------|

### 2. Service Area Competition (SAC)

**NOFO Number**: HRSA-26-005
**Purpose**: Ensure continued access to primary health care for communities currently served by Health Center Program

#### FY 2026 SAC Details

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Purpose | Competing continuation for existing service areas |
| Application System | 2-phase: Grants.gov + EHBs |
| Page Limit | 120 pages maximum |
| Awards | 1-year or 4-year periods |
| Start Date | May 1, 2026 |

#### Key Dates

| Milestone | Date |
|-----------|------|
| **Phase 1 (Grants.gov)** | **October 10, 2025** |
| **Phase 2 (EHBs)** | November 10, 2025 |
| Expected Awards | April 1, 2026 |
| Period of Performance Start | May 1, 2026 |

#### Eligibility Requirements

**Who May Apply**:
- Private, non-profit entities
- Public agencies in U.S. or territories
- Faith-based organizations
- Community-based organizations
- Native American tribes/tribal organizations

**Key Requirement**: Must propose to provide continuity of care in an area announced on the FY 2026 Service Area Announcement Table (SAAT)

#### Application Requirements

1. **Service Area Requirements**:
   - Serve at least 75% of SAAT Patient Target
   - Serve patients in ALL service types on SAAT (Medical, Dental, Mental Health, SUD, Vision, Enabling)
   - Cannot propose single age group or single health issue

2. **Site Operations**:
   - Verify sites operational within 120 days of award
   - Open all proposed sites within 1 year of Notice of Award
   - Sites must match Forms 5A (Services) and 5B (Sites)

3. **Priority Points** (Competing Continuation Only):
   - Patient Trend (5 points): Positive or neutral 3-year trend
   - PCMH Recognition (5 points): One or more sites with recognition
   - Requires no active conditions on Health Center Program compliance

#### Funding Restrictions

- No construction funding
- No vehicle purchases (except mobile units)
- Activities must directly relate to health center operations
- Indirect costs: Approved rate or 15% de minimis

### 3. Capital Development Grants

**Purpose**: Support health center expansion of capacity through construction, alteration, and renovation

#### Overview

Capital Development grants support health center efforts to expand capacity to provide primary and preventive health services to medically underserved communities.

**Contact**: BPHCCapital@hrsa.gov or 301-594-4300 (select 'Health Center' / 'Capital')

#### Types of Capital Projects

| Project Type | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Alteration/Renovation (A/R)** | Modifications to existing facility without adding square footage |
| **Construction/Expansion** | New construction or expanding existing facility footprint |
| **Equipment Only** | Acquisition of loose, moveable equipment not affixed to building |

#### Key Capital Programs

##### HRSA Loan Guarantee Program (LGP)

- **Established**: 1997
- **Coverage**: Up to 80% of principal amount
- **Purpose**: Construction, renovation, modernization of health center facilities
- **Partnership**: Private sector lenders working with HRSA

##### American Rescue Plan - Capital (ARP-Capital) (FY 2021)

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Total Awarded | Nearly $1 billion |
| Award Date | September 28, 2021 |
| Purpose | Construction, expansion, alteration, renovation, capital improvements |
| Source | American Rescue Plan Act |

**ARP-Capital Restrictions**:
- Cannot fund operating costs (services, clinical FTEs, rent, mortgage)
- Cannot address facility needs that are lessor's obligation
- Cannot supplant other federal/state/local/private funds
- Must be separate from other existing construction projects

##### Capital Assistance for Disaster Response (CADRE)

| Track | Purpose | Maximum Award |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| Track 3 | Major Construction-Related | Up to $1,000,000 |

**Track 3 Details**:
- Alteration/renovation and/or construction/expansion
- May include equipment
- At one eligible service delivery site

#### Eligibility

**General Requirement**: Eligibility limited to currently-funded health centers under the Health Center Program (H80 award recipients)

## Application Process Overview

### Registration Requirements

1. **SAM.gov Registration**
   - Required for all federal grants
   - Allow 4+ weeks for initial registration
   - Must match EHBs and Grants.gov information

2. **Grants.gov Registration**
   - Electronic submission portal
   - Organization and individual accounts needed

3. **HRSA Electronic Handbooks (EHBs)**
   - Phase 2 submissions for SAC/NAP
   - Ongoing compliance documentation
   - Performance reporting

### Two-Phase Submission Process

**Phase 1 (Grants.gov)**:
- Standard forms (SF-424, etc.)
- Application narratives
- Budget documentation

**Phase 2 (EHBs)**:
- Form 5A: Services Provided
- Form 5B: Service Sites
- Form 5C: Other Activities
- Additional program-specific forms
