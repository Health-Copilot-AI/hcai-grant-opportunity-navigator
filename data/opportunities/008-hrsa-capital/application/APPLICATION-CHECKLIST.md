## Pre-Application Requirements

### Eligibility Verification

- [ ] **Organizational Status**: Confirm 501(c)(3) status OR public agency eligibility
- [ ] **Program-Specific Eligibility**:
  - [ ] **Capital Development**: Must be H80 award recipient (existing health center) OR partner with one
  - [ ] **New Access Points**: Can be new organization meeting Health Center Program requirements
  - [ ] **Service Area Competition**: Must apply for area listed on Service Area Announcement Table (SAAT)
- [ ] **Service Area Requirements**: Verify proposed area qualifies as Medically Underserved Area (MUA) or Medically Underserved Population (MUP)
- [ ] **Geographic Restrictions**:
  - [ ] Urban sites: Within 15 miles of other organization sites (NAP)
  - [ ] Rural sites: Within 30 miles of other organization sites (NAP)
- [ ] **Tax Status**: Current IRS determination letter available
- [ ] **Financial Standing**: No debarment or suspension from federal programs

### Registration Requirements (CRITICAL - START EARLY)

#### 1. SAM.gov Registration (FIRST - Allow 7-10 business days)

- [ ] Obtain Unique Entity Identifier (UEI) - 12-character alphanumeric ID
- [ ] Gather required information:
  - [ ] Tax ID number (Employer Identification Number)
  - [ ] Business start date
  - [ ] Congressional district
  - [ ] Physical and mailing addresses (with 9-digit ZIP codes)
- [ ] Designate Electronic Business Point of Contact (EBiz POC) with email
- [ ] Complete registration at [SAM.gov](https://www.sam.gov)
- [ ] Wait for validation email (1-2 weeks)
- [ ] **Maintain active registration**: Must renew annually throughout grant lifecycle

**Timeline**: Average 7-10 business days after information entered
**URL**: https://www.sam.gov
**Reference**: [HRSA Mandatory Registrations Guide](https://www.hrsa.gov/grants/apply-for-a-grant/complete-mandatory-registrations)

#### 2. Grants.gov Registration (SECOND - Allow 1-2 business days after SAM.gov)

- [ ] Wait 1 day after SAM.gov submission
- [ ] Create Login.gov account
- [ ] Designate Authorized Organization Representative (AOR)
- [ ] AOR completes Grants.gov registration
- [ ] Verify UEI matches SAM.gov exactly
- [ ] Test workspace creation and document upload

**Timeline**: 1-2 business days after SAM.gov completion
**URL**: https://www.grants.gov
**Technical Support**: Available through Grants.gov help desk

#### 3. HRSA Electronic Handbooks (EHBs) Registration (THIRD - Quick but requires SAM.gov)

- [ ] Access HRSA EHBs at [grants.hrsa.gov](https://grants.hrsa.gov/)
- [ ] Project Director (PD) creates account and registers to grant
- [ ] Financial Reporting Administrator (FRA) registers using Payment Management System (PMS) PIN
- [ ] Verify information matches SAM.gov and Grants.gov EXACTLY
- [ ] Test access to forms and submission capabilities
- [ ] Review EHBs Knowledge Base for guidance

**Critical**: Information must be identical across SAM.gov, Grants.gov, and EHBs
**Timeline**: Quick registration but wait until SAM.gov is complete
**URL**: https://grants.hrsa.gov/
**Support**: 877-464-4772 or [help.hrsa.gov](https://help.hrsa.gov/)

### Internal Approvals

- [ ] Department/College approval obtained (HWCOM Dean's office)
- [ ] Office of Research notification submitted
- [ ] Budget office consulted for federal indirect cost rate
- [ ] General Counsel review (if subcontracts/partnerships involved)
- [ ] IRB determination obtained (if applicable to proposed services)
- [ ] Environmental assessment clearance (Capital Development only)

## Application Materials Needed

### TWO-PHASE SUBMISSION PROCESS

**Phase 1: Grants.gov Submission**
- Deadline: 11:59 PM ET on NOFO deadline date
- System: Grants.gov
- Content: SF-424 forms, narratives, budget

**Phase 2: HRSA EHBs Submission**
- Deadline: 5:00 PM ET on NOFO deadline date (typically 1 month after Phase 1)
- System: HRSA Electronic Handbooks
- Content: Forms 5A/5B/5C, program-specific forms

## Key Messaging Points

- **Health Center Program Mission Alignment**: Position project as expanding access to comprehensive primary health care for medically underserved populations, directly advancing HRSA's mission of eliminating health disparities.

- **Mobile Health Innovation**: Emphasize mobile unit capabilities to overcome transportation barriers and reach populations where they live, work, and gather - a proven Health Center Program service delivery model.

- **Academic Medical Center Asset**: Leverage FIU HWCOM's unique position as medical education institution integrated with community health delivery, providing workforce pipeline and clinical excellence.

- **NeighborhoodHELP Track Record**: Demonstrate existing operational experience with mobile health delivery, patient engagement, and serving uninsured/underinsured populations in Miami-Dade County.

- **Community-Centered Governance**: Highlight community engagement approach and plans for consumer-majority board (51% requirement) ensuring patient voices drive program decisions.

- **Comprehensive Service Model**: Emphasize ability to provide full spectrum of required Health Center Program services (medical, dental, mental health, substance use disorder, enabling services).

- **Financial Sustainability**: Demonstrate clear path to 4-year sustainability through payer mix development, sliding fee scale implementation, and revenue diversification beyond federal grant funding.

- **Quality and Outcomes Focus**: Commit to PCMH recognition pursuit, UDS reporting excellence, and continuous quality improvement aligned with Health Center Program performance standards.

## Concept Development Suggestions

### Recommended Focus Areas (Based on HRSA Priorities)

1. **Expanding Primary Care Access in MUA/MUPs**: Mobile health units serving designated medically underserved areas in Miami-Dade County
2. **Integrated Behavioral Health Services**: Mental health and substance use disorder treatment integrated with primary care
3. **Social Determinants of Health**: Enabling services addressing housing, food security, transportation barriers
4. **Health Disparities Reduction**: Targeted services for uninsured, underinsured, communities of color
5. **Chronic Disease Management**: Hypertension, diabetes care in underserved communities (aligns with NHCI-HC if available)

### Potential Project Concepts

1. **NeighborhoodHELP Mobile Health Center Expansion**: Establish 2-3 new permanent mobile service sites in MUA-designated neighborhoods, providing comprehensive primary care, behavioral health, and enabling services to uninsured residents. Partner with existing FQHC for H80 eligibility and sustainability.

2. **Integrated Behavioral Health Mobile Units**: Deploy specialized mobile units providing co-located primary care and behavioral health services (mental health + substance use disorder treatment) addressing critical access gaps in underserved Miami-Dade communities.

3. **Academic Health Center Partnership Model**: Create innovative partnership between FIU HWCOM and established FQHC, leveraging medical education mission to staff mobile health services while expanding health center service capacity in designated underserved areas.

### Differentiation Strategy

- **Academic Medical Center Distinction**: Position as only academic medical center in region proposing mobile health center model, combining clinical excellence with medical education and research missions.

- **Established Mobile Infrastructure**: Leverage existing NeighborhoodHELP operational experience - not starting from zero, but scaling proven model.

- **Medical Education Integration**: Provide clinical training sites for future primary care workforce, addressing national health workforce shortage while delivering community services.

- **Technology-Enabled Care**: Emphasize health IT infrastructure, telehealth capabilities, and data systems supporting UDS reporting excellence.

- **Community Partnership Depth**: Demonstrate authentic community engagement and existing relationships with target populations and community-based organizations.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Registration Errors

- **Late Registration**: Waiting until NOFO release to start SAM.gov registration - process takes 4+ weeks
- **Mismatched Information**: Organization name, address, EIN not matching exactly across SAM.gov, Grants.gov, EHBs
- **Expired SAM.gov**: SAM.gov registration expires annually - check status well before deadline
- **Missing 9-Digit ZIP**: Using 5-digit ZIP codes instead of required 9-digit ZIP+4 format

### Application Content Errors

- **Ignoring Page Limits**: Exceeding narrative page limits results in pages being removed before review
- **Missing Required Sections**: Not addressing all NOFO-required sections in narrative
- **Inconsistent Data**: Services in Form 5A not matching sites in Form 5B
- **Weak Needs Assessment**: Failing to document MUA/MUP designation or community health needs data
- **No Sustainability Plan**: Not demonstrating clear path to financial viability beyond federal funding
- **Missing Consumer Majority**: Not ensuring 51% consumer representation on board governance

### Budget Errors

- **Unallowable Costs**: Including construction costs in operating grants, or operating costs in capital grants
- **Insufficient Justification**: Not explaining why each cost is necessary and how calculated
- **Incorrect Indirect Rate**: Using wrong rate or wrong cost base for indirect costs
- **Missing Match Documentation**: Not documenting required cost share or match (if applicable)

### Partnership Errors

- **No H80 Partnership**: Applying for Capital Development or SAC without existing health center status or partnership
- **Weak Letters of Support**: Generic letters not specific to project or community need
- **Unsigned MOUs**: Partnership agreements not signed by authorized representatives
- **Missing Form 8 Agreements**: Not documenting required hospital/specialty care arrangements

### Submission Errors

- **Last Minute Submission**: Submitting at deadline without time to fix technical problems
- **Wrong Deadline Time**: Confusing Phase 1 (11:59 PM) vs Phase 2 (5:00 PM) deadlines
- **Missing Confirmation**: Not saving submission confirmation receipts
- **Phase 2 Oversight**: Submitting Phase 1 but forgetting Phase 2 is required

### Technical Errors

- **Large File Sizes**: Exceeding Grants.gov file size limits for attachments
- **Wrong File Formats**: Using non-compliant file formats (use PDF for most attachments)
- **Incomplete Validation**: Not running and fixing all validation errors before submission
- **Browser Compatibility**: Using incompatible browser for EHBs submission
