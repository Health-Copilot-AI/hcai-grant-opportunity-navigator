## Mission & Priorities

### Mission Statement

> "Transform the health of the communities we serve, one person at a time" through partnerships and philanthropy that dismantle barriers to health using holistic approaches.

*Source: https://www.centene.com/who-we-are/centene-foundation.html (Retrieved: 2026-02-02)*

### Strategic Priorities

1. **Healthcare Access**: Focus on social determinants of health (SDOH), primary care access, behavioral health integration, and chronic disease management for underserved populations.

2. **Social Services Integration**: Fund programs addressing housing stability, food security, transportation access, and economic mobility as critical health determinants.

3. **Education & Workforce Development**: Support health literacy programs, STEM education initiatives, workforce development, and youth development programs that build pathways to healthcare careers.

4. **Long-Term Partnership Model**: Preference for sustained, multi-year partnerships over transactional one-time grants, with emphasis on growing relationships and deepening impact.

5. **Medicaid Population Focus**: Priority for programs serving individuals reliant on government-sponsored healthcare (Medicaid, Medicare, marketplace) in communities where Centene health plans operate.

### Target Populations

- Medicaid-eligible individuals and families
- Medicare beneficiaries
- Health insurance marketplace enrollees
- Underserved and vulnerable communities
- Populations in Centene health plan service areas (critical)

## Decision Making

### Review Process

1. **Initial Screening**: Foundation staff review applications for eligibility and strategic alignment
2. **Due Diligence**: Organizational capacity assessment, financial review, exclusion list verification
3. **Program Assessment**: Evaluation against review criteria and priority areas
4. **Board Review**: Recommendations presented to Board of Directors for approval
5. **Site Visits**: May be conducted for larger grants or new partnerships
6. **Contract Execution**: Award agreements finalized with approved grantees

**2026+ Invitation-Only Model**: Local Centene health plans identify prospective grantees aligned with strategic goals; invited organizations receive application support and guidance through process.

### Review Criteria

- **Mission Alignment**: Clear connection to healthcare access, social services, or education priorities
- **Population Served**: Focus on Medicaid/Medicare populations and underserved communities
- **Community Impact**: Demonstrated ability to address health barriers and improve outcomes
- **Partnership Potential**: Capacity for long-term, growing relationship vs. one-time engagement
- **Geographic Relevance**: Location in Centene health plan service area (strongly preferred)
- **Organizational Capacity**: Proven track record, financial stability, evaluation capabilities
- **Innovation**: Novel approaches to addressing social determinants of health

### Decision Timeline

Typical review timeline: 3-6 months from application deadline to decision notification.

Board meets periodically to review grant recommendations.

### Award Notification

Grantees notified via email and phone. Non-funded applicants receive notification via email.

## Strategic Notes

1. **Invitation-Only Urgency**: With 2026 transition to invitation-only model, establishing relationship with Sunshine Health (Centene's Florida subsidiary) is **critical and time-sensitive**. Fall 2025 may represent final open application opportunity.

2. **Sunshine Health Connection**: Florida-based applications must emphasize alignment with Sunshine Health priorities and service to Medicaid populations in Miami-Dade County. Direct outreach to Sunshine Health leadership should precede any foundation application.

3. **Partnership Over Projects**: Centene explicitly favors long-term partnerships that grow over time. Application should propose multi-year vision with potential for expansion rather than single-year project. Frame initial grant as entry point to sustained collaboration.

4. **SDOH Comprehensive Approach**: FIU Thrive's integration of social determinant screening, navigation, and closed-loop referrals directly addresses Centene's priority areas (food, housing, transportation, social services). This is a strong differentiator.

5. **Medicaid ROI Potential**: Position NeighborhoodHELP as infrastructure serving Centene's customer base. Emphasize care coordination, preventive care access, emergency department diversion, and cost savings potential for Medicaid managed care plans.

6. **Academic Value-Add**: Highlight workforce development dimension (medical student/resident training in community health, SDOH screening) as unique benefit of academic partnership vs. traditional community health center grant.

7. **Data-Driven Outcomes**: Centene will expect robust evaluation and outcome reporting. FIU Thrive platform capabilities for data collection, tracking, and outcomes measurement should be prominently featured.

8. **Geographic Strategy**: While Centene operates nationally, Florida grants are feasible given Sunshine Health presence. Review prior Florida grants in 990-PF filings to identify precedent and successful applicant profiles.

9. **Grant Size Positioning**: Average grant is $235,885; median ~$67,000. Request should be calibrated to demonstrated organizational capacity and project scope. Modest initial request with expansion potential may be strategic.

10. **Event Sponsorship Alternative**: If formal grant application timeline doesn't align, event sponsorships are accepted year-round. Could be relationship-building vehicle (e.g., sponsor NeighborhoodHELP community health event to establish Centene connection).
