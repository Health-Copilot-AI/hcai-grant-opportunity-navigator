## Mission and Vision

### Mission Statement
The Gilead Foundation pursues **"Health for All"** by working to create a thriving health ecosystem with health prosperity for all. The organization takes a holistic approach to addressing inequities in communities, classrooms, and workplaces that create barriers to healthy, fulfilling lives.

### Strategic Philosophy
Gilead recognizes that addressing public health challenges requires partnership beyond medicine alone. According to VP of Corporate Giving Korab Zuka: "It will take more than medicine to end some of the greatest public health crises" - the company focuses on "partnership with communities most directly impacted."

### Three Pillars of Giving

The signature initiative addressing social determinants of health with emphasis on education equity.

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Focus** | Education equity as a social determinant of health |
| **Approach** | Multi-year, significant funding for capacity building |
| **Priority Populations** | Black students, underrepresented communities |
| **Grant Duration** | 3-year funding cycles |
| **Model** | Cohort-based learning community |

## Application Process

### Foundation Grants (Creating Possible Fund, Building Community)
1. Submit Letter of Interest (LOI) through online portal
2. Complete guided questionnaire
3. Create account or log in at grant portal
4. **Portal URL**: https://fsrequests.com/gilead
5. **Contact**: GileadFoundation@gilead.com

### Corporate Grants (Therapeutic Area Funding)

**North America Process:**
1. Review guidelines at Apply for Funding page
2. Obtain Federal Tax ID (U.S. organizations)
3. Access online portals:
   - Grants: FoundationSource portal
   - Sponsorships: Steeprock portal
4. Submit required information:
   - Project dates/locations
   - Agenda
   - Budget breakdown
   - Target audience
   - CME details (if applicable)
5. Finalize and submit application

**Deadline**: Rolling (year-round acceptance)

### Eligibility Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Organization Type** | Nonprofits, tax-exempt 501(c)(3) organizations only |
| **Tax ID** | U.S. federal tax ID required (Canadian orgs exempt) |
| **Documentation** | 501(c)(3) IRS determination letter |
| **Individual Applicants** | Not eligible - organizations only |
| **Fiscal Sponsors** | Accepted for organizations without 501(c)(3) status |

## Strategic Alignment with FIU Programs

### NeighborhoodHELP Alignment

| Gilead Priority | NHELP Capability |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Testing/screening in communities | Mobile health units, community-based care |
| Patient education | Family-centered health education |

### FIU Thrive Alignment

| Gilead Priority | Thrive Capability |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| SDOH screening | AI-powered social needs assessment |
| Health equity | Vulnerable population identification |

### Recommended Positioning
Frame applications around:
1. **SDOH screening and intervention** through Thrive platform
2. **Community-based delivery** through NHELP infrastructure
3. **Health equity outcomes** with measurable patient impact
4. **Workforce development** through student/CHW training
5. **Technology innovation** with AI-enhanced assessments
