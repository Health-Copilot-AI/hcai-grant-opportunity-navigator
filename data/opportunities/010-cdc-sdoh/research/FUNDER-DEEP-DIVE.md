## Mission and Strategic Priorities

The National Center for Chronic Disease Prevention and Health Promotion (NCCDPHP) is CDC's primary center dedicated to preventing chronic diseases and promoting healthy behaviors. NCCDPHP invests in communities to reduce chronic diseases and equitably address social determinants of health (SDOH).

### Core NCCDPHP SDOH Domains

NCCDPHP focuses on five key SDOH priority areas:

| Abbreviation | Domain | Description |
|--------------|--------|-------------|
| **BE** | Built Environment | Physical structures and infrastructure affecting health access |
| **CCL** | Community-Clinical Linkages | Connections between community resources and healthcare systems |
| **FNS** | Food and Nutrition Security | Access to affordable, nutritious food |
| **SC** | Social Connectedness | Social support networks and community engagement |
| **TFP** | Tobacco-Free Policy | Policy interventions to reduce tobacco exposure |

## Key Funding Programs

### 1. SDOH Accelerator Plans (Planning Grants)

**Program Name**: Closing the Gap with Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) Accelerator Plans
**Purpose**: Support development of implementation-ready SDOH plans

#### Program History

| Fiscal Year | NOFO Number | Recipients Funded | Notes |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| 2021-2022 | CDC-RFA-DP21-2111 | 20 recipients | Initial launch |
| 2022-2023 | CDC-RFA-DP22-2210 | 36 recipients | Expanded funding |
| 2023-2024 | CDC-RFA-DP23-0077 | 15 recipients | Most recent cycle |
| **TOTAL** | - | **71 recipients** | Since program inception |

#### Funding Details

- **Award Amount**: Up to $125,000 per award
- **Duration**: 1 year (recipients funded once)
- **Maximum Awards**: Up to 20 awards per funding cycle
- **Geographic Distribution**:
  - At least one territory funded
  - At least one tribe funded
  - No more than 3 state/local applicants per HHS region

#### Eligibility Requirements

**Eligible Applicants**:
- State governments (or bona fide agents)
- Local governments (or bona fide agents)
- Territorial governments (or bona fide agents)
- County, city, or township governments
- Special district governments
- Federally recognized Native American tribal governments
- Native American tribal organizations

#### Application Requirements

**Leadership Team Requirements**:
- At least 2 community members with understanding of populations served
- At least 5 leaders from different sectors to support plan development
- Multisectoral partnership approach required

**Plan Requirements**:
- Must focus on at least 2 of the 5 NCCDPHP SDOH domains
- Must be implementation-ready upon completion
- Must target chronic disease outcomes in populations experiencing health disparities

---

### 2. ACTion Grants (Implementation Grants)

**Program Name**: Addressing Conditions to Improve Population Health (ACTion)
**Purpose**: Fund implementation of established SDOH plans

#### Program Details

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Award Period | 3 years (2023-2026) |
| Award Amount | Up to $500,000 per year |
| Total Per Award | Up to $1,500,000 over 3 years |
| Recipients | 5 jurisdictions funded |
| Eligible Applicants | Prior SDOH Accelerator Plan recipients |

#### Requirements

- Must implement policy, systems, and environmental change interventions
- Must address at least one of four NCCDPHP SDOH domains (BE, CCL, FNS, SC)
- Must have completed an approved SDOH Accelerator Plan
- Must focus on reducing chronic disease disparities

## Application Requirements Summary

### Required Components

1. **Organizational Capacity**
   - Demonstrated experience with population health initiatives
   - Track record of multi-sector collaboration
   - Capacity for data collection and reporting

2. **Leadership Team Structure**
   - Community member representation (minimum 2)
   - Multi-sector leaders (minimum 5)
   - Clear governance structure

3. **SDOH Plan Elements**
   - Selection of 2+ SDOH domains
   - Evidence-based intervention strategies
   - Implementation timeline
   - Sustainability plan

4. **Target Population**
   - Defined geographic area or jurisdiction
   - Documented health disparities
   - Chronic disease burden data
