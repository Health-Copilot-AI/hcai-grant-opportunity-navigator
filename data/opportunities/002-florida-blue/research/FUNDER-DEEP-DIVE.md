## Executive Summary

The Florida Blue Foundation is the philanthropic affiliate of Florida Blue (Blue Cross Blue Shield of Florida), part of the GuideWell family of companies. With **$450 million in assets** and annual giving exceeding **$17 million**, it is one of Florida's largest health-focused corporate foundations. The Foundation has operated for **25 years** (since 2001), reaching approximately **10 million Floridians annually** through community-based investments.

**Key Takeaways for Grant Seekers:**
- Strong focus on AI-powered health solutions (explicitly requested in maternal health grants)
- Preference for under-resourced and rural communities
- Multi-year grants (3-4 years) provide sustained funding
- Track record matters - current/prior grantees have significant advantage
- Online portal-based application system

## Mission, Vision, and Values

### Mission Statement

> "To help people and communities achieve better health"

### Strategic Focus

The Foundation organizes its work around three strategic pillars:

1. **Community Investments** - Delivering resources and programming to improve community health through targeted grants
2. **Community Leadership & Education** - Building partnerships and serving as a thought leader on community health issues
3. **Employee Engagement** - Supporting volunteer efforts and corporate giving initiatives

### Drivers of Health (Core Focus Areas)

The Foundation has identified three "Drivers of Health" as their primary investment areas:

1. **Food Security** - Addressing hunger and access to nutritious food
2. **Mental Well-Being** - Promoting mental health services and professional development
3. **Health Equity** - Reducing disparities and ensuring equitable access to care

## Grant Programs and Funding Areas

### Current Active Programs (as of February 2026)

| Program | Status | Award Range | Duration |
|---------|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Healthy Communities Grants** | Open (rolling) | Varies | Varies |
| **Food Security** | 2025 cycle closed | ~$375K average | 3-4 years |
| **Maternal Health** | 2026 cycle closed | ~$388K average | 3-4 years |
| **Mental Well-Being** | 2025 cycle closed | ~$343K average | 3-4 years |

### Program Details

#### 1. Maternal Health Grants

**Purpose:** Address Florida's maternal mortality crisis (rate higher than national average; 19.4% of counties are "maternity care deserts")

**Priority Solutions:**
- Improve maternal health outcomes for those with chronic conditions (hypertension, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, obesity)
- Perinatal and postpartum maternal mental health support

**Special Considerations:**
- **AI-powered solutions** (chatbots, predictive analytics) - explicitly requested
- Services in under-resourced communities
- Rural county coverage

**2025 Round 1 Awards:** $3.1 million to 8 nonprofits
**2025 Round 2 Pool:** ~$3 million earmarked

#### 2. Mental Well-Being Grants

**Purpose:** Increase access to mental well-being services and support

**Target Populations:**
- Underserved, underinsured, and low-income individuals
- Rural communities
- Youth and teens

**Focus Areas:**
- Programs that promote whole-person health
- Address unmet mental health needs
- Foster a healthier and more resilient Florida
- Training mental health professionals (pipeline development)

**2024 Awards:** $2.4 million to 7 organizations
**5-Year Investment:** $18.9 million

#### 3. Food Security Grants

**Purpose:** Reduce food insecurity and address diet-related chronic conditions

**Target Approach:**
- Holistic approach to food insecurity
- Access to affordable healthy foods for low-income families
- Focus on food deserts
- Integration with health outcomes

**2025 Awards:** $3 million to 8 organizations
**4-Year Investment:** $16+ million

#### 4. Sapphire Awards

**Purpose:** Recognize excellence and innovation in community health

**Award Categories:**
- Individual
- Organization
- Program

**Award Amounts:** Total pool of $525,000 annually
- First Place: $100,000
- Second Place: $50,000
- Third Place: $25,000

**History:** Since 2005, 157 Sapphire Awards totaling $8 million have been presented

## Application Process and Requirements

### General Eligibility

Based on program descriptions and grant awards:
- 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
- Florida-based or serving Florida communities
- Documented expertise and experience in the relevant focus area
- Evidence-based, sustainable initiatives

### Standard Requirements (Typical)

1. **IRS Determination Letter** - Confirming 501(c)(3) status
2. **Bylaws and Governing Documents** - Board operation and governance
3. **Employer Identification Number (EIN)** - Required for all filings
4. **Financial Statements** - Budgets, prior financial reports
5. **Program Description** - Clear articulation of proposed activities
6. **Outcomes Framework** - Measurable goals and evaluation plan

### Application Portal

- **URL:** floridablue.smartsimple.com
- **Features:**
  - View current funding opportunities
  - Check application status
  - Submit reports
  - Provide required documentation

### Application Tips

Based on successful grantee profiles:

1. **Demonstrate Community Impact** - Quantify reach and outcomes
2. **Show Innovation** - Especially for AI-powered solutions
3. **Address Health Equity** - Focus on under-resourced communities
4. **Evidence-Based Approach** - Reference research and best practices
5. **Sustainability Plan** - How will impact continue post-grant?
6. **Partnership Model** - Collaboration with community organizations
7. **Geographic Specificity** - Clear county/region identification

## Key Decision-Making Factors

Based on analysis of successful grantees and Foundation priorities:

### Strong Indicators

1. **Innovation** - Novel approaches, especially technology-enabled
2. **AI Integration** - Explicitly requested for maternal health
3. **Community Reach** - Significant population served
4. **Under-resourced Focus** - Low-income, underinsured, rural populations
5. **Health Outcomes** - Clear connection to improved health
6. **Sustainability** - Long-term viability beyond grant period
7. **Track Record** - Prior successful grants with Foundation

### Geographic Preferences

- Statewide reach valued
- Rural counties receive special consideration
- Food deserts specifically mentioned
- "Maternity care deserts" are priority

### Population Priorities

- Low-income families
- Underserved and underinsured individuals
- Children and youth
- Seniors (especially with chronic conditions)
- Pregnant and postpartum women
- Individuals with chronic conditions
