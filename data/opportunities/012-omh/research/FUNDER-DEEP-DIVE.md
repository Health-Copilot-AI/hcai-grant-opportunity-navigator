## Mission and Strategic Priorities

The Office of Minority Health is dedicated to improving the health of racial and ethnic minority and American Indian and Alaska Native populations through the development of health policies and programs that help eliminate health disparities.

### Core Focus Areas

- Reducing health disparities in minority populations
- Improving access to care for underserved communities
- Supporting evidence-based interventions for health equity
- Building capacity in minority-serving organizations
- Advancing health equity research and data

## Key OMH Grant Programs

### 1. COPHI - Community-Based Perinatal Health Initiative

**Full Name**: Healthy Families Community-Based Perinatal Health Initiative (COPHI)
**CFDA**: 93.137 (Community Programs to Improve Minority Health)

#### Program Overview

COPHI develops innovative models for integrating community-based maternal support services (COMSS) into perinatal systems of care.

#### FY 2023 Awards

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Total Awarded | $13+ million |
| Number of Awards | 11 organizations |
| Award Range | $975,000 - $1,250,000 annually |
| Duration | Up to 4 years |
| Optional Extension | Competitive 5th year for sustainability transition |
| Project Start | September 30, 2023 |

#### Health Disparities Addressed

| Population | Disparity |
|------------|-----------|
| Black women | 3x more likely to die from pregnancy-related complications vs. non-Hispanic White women |
| American Indian/Alaska Native women | 2x more likely to die from pregnancy-related complications |
| Hispanic women | Higher rates of severe maternal morbidity |

#### Community-Based Maternal Support Services (COMSS)

COMSS addresses social determinants of health including:
- Health literacy
- Pregnancy, childbirth, and parenting education
- Cultural and linguistic diversity
- Exposure to trauma
- Housing stability
- Food security
- Transportation access

**Service Providers**: Trained doulas and community health workers provide services during:
- Pregnancy
- Labor and delivery
- Post-partum period

#### FY 2023 COPHI Awardee Locations

Known geographic coverage includes:
- **Louisiana**: Orleans, Jefferson, Plaquemines, and St. Bernard Parishes
- **New Jersey**: Mercer, Monmouth, and Ocean Counties
- **Puerto Rico**

**Note**: Complete list of 11 awardee organizations available at OMH website.

### 2. State Partnership Initiative (SPI)

**Full Name**: State Partnership Initiative to Address Health Disparities
**CFDA**: 93.296 (State Partnership Grant Program to Improve Minority Health)

#### Program Purpose

Demonstrate that partnerships between:
- State offices of minority health/health equity AND state health agencies, OR
- Tribes AND tribal health agencies/organizations

Can efficiently and effectively improve health outcomes and address health disparities in selected geographical "hotspots."

#### Program Goals

1. **Health Disparities Profile**: Produce profile on 1-3 Healthy People leading health indicator topics for geographical hotspots
2. **Community Interventions**: Implement interventions using strategies to improve health outcomes
3. **Publication/Dissemination**: Publish results and/or produce scholarly articles comparing baseline data to outcomes

#### Health Focus Areas

- Adult/child immunization
- Asthma
- Cancer
- Diabetes
- Heart disease and stroke
- HIV
- Infant mortality
- Mental health

#### Example SPI Projects

**Minnesota**:
- Focus: Infant mortality in U.S.-born African American community in Hennepin County
- Approach: Health equity narrative development
- Goal: Identify and prioritize efforts addressing factors contributing to infant mortality

**Massachusetts**:
- Focus: Oral health equity for underserved Black and Latino children
- Activities: Connect children to mobile dental services and oral health homes
- Outputs: Peer-reviewed articles, conference presentations, Statewide Oral Health Equity plan

### 4. Partnership Grant Programs

**CFDA**: 93.137 (Community Programs to Improve Minority Health)

#### Purpose

Demonstrate that multi-partner collaborations addressing SDOH with nationwide or regional reach can:

1. Improve access to and utilization of care by racial/ethnic minority and disadvantaged populations
2. Increase health workforce diversity through high school/undergraduate programs
3. Increase data availability regarding health disparities

#### State Partnership vs. National Partnership

| Type | Lead Entity | Scope |
|------|-------------|-------|
| State Partnership | State OMH/health equity office + state health agency | State/regional |
| National Partnership | Multi-partner collaboration | Nationwide/regional |

## CMS Office of Minority Health Programs

### Minority Research Grant Program (MRGP) 2025

**Administering Office**: CMS Office of Minority Health
**Funding Opportunity Number**: CMS-1W1-25-001

#### Key Dates

| Milestone | Date |
|-----------|------|
| Letter of Intent Deadline | March 6, 2025 |
| **Application Deadline** | **April 1, 2025** |
| Award Notification | September 30, 2025 |
| Project Start | September 30, 2025 or later |

#### Funding Details

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Total Anticipated Funding | $1,275,000 |
| Number of Awards | Up to 6 grants |
| Maximum Per Award | $212,500 over 2 years |
| Cost Share Required | 1% of total project cost |

#### Purpose

- Support health equity research
- Encourage innovative health services research
- Contribute to improving health outcomes for minority populations
- Test solutions in existing CMS programs, policies, or practices affecting SDOH

#### Eligible Applicants

Public and private higher education institutions that are:
- Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs)
- Hispanic-serving institutions
- Asian American and Native American Pacific Islander-serving institutions
- Tribal colleges
- Similar organizations serving underrepresented communities

#### Focus Areas

Applicants must address:
- Significant health equity issues
- Innovative solutions
- One or more SDOH areas:
  - Housing stability
  - Community-clinical linkages
  - Food security
  - Transportation needs

## Funding Search Services

### OMH Information Specialist Service

OMH provides specialized funding searches through Information Specialists.

#### Eligible Organizations for Search Service

- Non-profit organizations with 501(c) status
- Faith-based organizations
- Universities
- Hospitals/medical centers
- Local/county departments
- Researchers and students

**Note**: Most grants are NOT offered to individuals, private companies, or small businesses

#### Search Process

1. Submit questionnaire with detailed organizational information
2. Information Specialists search federal, state, local, and foundation opportunities
3. Results provided within 5-7 business days

## Application Requirements Summary

### Common Elements Across OMH Programs

1. **Organizational Capacity**
   - Track record with minority health populations
   - Evidence-based intervention experience
   - Data collection and evaluation capability

2. **Community Engagement**
   - Partnership letters
   - Community advisory involvement
   - Cultural competency demonstration

3. **Target Population**
   - Clear definition of minority/disadvantaged population
   - Health disparity documentation
   - Geographic focus area

4. **Intervention Design**
   - Evidence-based approach
   - SDOH integration
   - Measurable outcomes

5. **Sustainability Plan**
   - Post-grant continuation strategy
   - Partnership sustainability
   - Funding diversification
