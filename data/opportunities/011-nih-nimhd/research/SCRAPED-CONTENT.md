### Full Content Summary

#### Program Overview

**Title:** Community Level Interventions to Improve Minority Health and Reduce Health Disparities (R01 - Clinical Trial Optional)

**Funding Organization:** National Institute on Minority Health and Health Disparities (NIMHD)

#### Purpose Statement

This initiative supports research developing and testing interventions at the community level to enhance minority health outcomes and address health disparities, moving beyond individual-focused approaches to structural, multi-level interventions.

#### Eligible Organizations

- Higher education institutions (public and private)
- Nonprofit organizations with/without 501(c)(3) status
- For-profit organizations and small businesses
- Local, state, and tribal governments
- Faith-based and community-based organizations

**Not eligible:**
- Non-U.S. entities or foreign components of U.S. organizations

#### Budget & Timeline

- **Annual Budget Limit:** $1,000,000 direct costs (excluding consortium F&A)
- **Project Period:** Maximum 5 years
- **Activity Code:** R01 Research Project Grant

#### Key Due Dates (While Active)

- **Letter of Intent:** 30 days before application deadline
- **Application Deadlines:** October 5, 2024; October 5, 2025; October 5, 2026

#### Required Application Components

**Core Sections:**
- Research strategy identifying communities and health disparity populations
- Justification of community-level intervention design
- Conceptual model describing "hypothesized pathways between the community-level intervention, community-level determinants, and health outcomes"
- Data sources for assessing community-level determinant changes
- Data analysis plan for multi-level effects
- Community partner roles specification
- Letters of support from community collaborators
- Data Management and Sharing Plan
- Human Subjects documentation (if applicable)

#### Non-Responsive Applications

Applications will not be reviewed if they:
- Lack focus on NIH-designated health disparity populations
- Don't test prospective community-level interventions
- Use only individual-level data
- Constitute individual-level randomized clinical trials
- Exclude community partners as compensated key personnel

#### Review Criteria (Scored)

1. **Significance:** Problem importance, prior research rigor, potential impact on field and practice
2. **Investigator(s):** Researcher qualifications, complementary expertise, community partnership clarity
3. **Innovation:** Novel theoretical approaches, methodologies, or interventions
4. **Approach:** Well-reasoned strategy, methodology addressing prior research weaknesses, robust study design
5. **Environment:** Adequate institutional support, resources, and community endorsement

#### Research Focus Areas

Special interest includes:
- Increasing affordable healthy food access and physical activity opportunities
- Changing health-promoting behavioral norms
- Improving community attitudes toward stigmatized groups
- Promoting screening and self-management for chronic diseases
- Supporting aging in place and independent living
- Facilitating community reintegration post-incarceration
- Preventing injury and violence

### Key Content

#### Mission Statement

"The mission of NIMHD is to lead scientific research in humans to improve minority health and reduce health disparities in the United States and its territories."

The institute supports research across clinical care, health services, community health, data science, and behavioral sciences.

#### Leadership

- **Scientific Director**: Kelvin Choi, Ph.D., M.P.H. (appointed November 17, 2024, to lead the Division of Intramural Research)

#### Organizational Structure

NIMHD includes:
- Division of Intramural Research (DIR)
- Extramural Research Programs
- Native Hawaiian and Pacific Islander (NHPI) Health Research Office
- Advisory Council (National Advisory Council on Minority Health and Health Disparities)

#### Strategic Focus Areas

1. Clinical care, health services, and health systems research
2. Community health and population science
3. Data science and integrative biological/behavioral sciences
4. Research addressing health disparities in designated populations

#### Current Funding Opportunities Listed

- Ruth L. Kirschstein National Research Service Awards (predoctoral and postdoctoral fellowships)
- Career development grants
- Individual fellowship grants
- Loan repayment programs
- Early Career Investigator Awards

#### Key Resources Mentioned

- **SCHARE**: Cloud-based population science platform
- **HDPulse**: Online health disparities research resource
- **PhenX SDOH Toolkit**: Social determinants of health measurement protocols
