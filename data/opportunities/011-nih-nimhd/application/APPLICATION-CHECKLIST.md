## Application Materials Needed

### Narrative Components

- [ ] **Specific Aims** (1 page)
  - Clear statement of objectives
  - Specific, testable hypotheses
  - Health disparity population focus explicit
  - Innovation highlighted

- [ ] **Research Strategy** (12 pages total)

  - [ ] **Significance** (approx. 2-3 pages)
    - Importance of problem
    - Gaps in current knowledge
    - Impact on health disparities
    - Policy/practice implications

  - [ ] **Innovation** (approx. 1-2 pages)
    - Novel concepts or approaches
    - New methodologies
    - Technology applications (e.g., Thrive AI)

  - [ ] **Approach** (approx. 7-9 pages)
    - Study design and rationale
    - Population and setting
    - Intervention description
    - Outcome measures
    - Data collection procedures
    - Analysis plan (multi-level)
    - Timeline
    - Potential problems and solutions

- [ ] **Project Summary/Abstract** (30 lines max)
  - Structured format
  - Plain language for lay reviewers

- [ ] **Project Narrative** (2-3 sentences)
  - Public health relevance
  - Plain language

- [ ] **Bibliography & References** (no page limit)
  - Complete citations
  - Recent and seminal works

### Budget Documents

- [ ] **Budget** (Modular or Detailed)
  - Modular: $250K or less direct costs/year (in $25K modules)
  - Detailed: Over $250K direct costs/year
  - Template: SF424 (R&R) Budget forms

- [ ] **Budget Justification**
  - Personnel effort and roles
  - Consultant justification
  - Equipment needs
  - Supplies breakdown
  - Travel justification
  - Subaward justification
  - Other costs explanation

- [ ] **Indirect Cost Documentation**
  - FIU negotiated F&A rate agreement

- [ ] **Subaward Budgets** (for community partners)
  - Budget forms for each subaward

## Key Messaging Points

- **Health Disparities Focus**: Explicitly target NIH-designated health disparity populations (racial/ethnic minorities, low SES, rural)

- **FIU-HDI Relationship**: Reference existing NIMHD S21 award to establish institutional credibility

- **Community Partnership**: Emphasize authentic community engagement, not token involvement - community partners as key personnel

- **Thrive Innovation**: Position AI-enhanced SDOH screening as novel approach to persistent disparities

- **NHELP Platform**: Describe established mobile health infrastructure as ideal community research platform

- **Implementation Science**: Frame as implementation research testing real-world intervention, not basic science

- **Multi-Level Approach**: Use NIMHD Research Framework - individual, interpersonal, community, societal levels

## Submission Instructions

1. **Register** - Ensure all registrations current (eRA Commons, SAM.gov, Grants.gov)
2. **Complete Application** - All forms in ASSIST or institutional system
3. **Validate** - Run system validation checks
4. **Route for Approval** - Internal FIU routing and AOR approval
5. **Submit** - Via Grants.gov by deadline (5:00 PM local time)
6. **Track** - Monitor eRA Commons for status updates
7. **Save** - Confirmation emails and tracking numbers

**Submission Portal**: https://grants.gov/
**Deadline Time Zone**: Applicant's local time (5:00 PM)

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Late Registrations** - Start SAM.gov/eRA Commons 6+ weeks early
2. **Weak Community Partnership** - Must be authentic, not token involvement
3. **Individual-Level Focus Only** - Must include community-level data/analysis
4. **Missing Disparity Focus** - Health disparity population must be explicit
5. **Biosketch Format** - Use current NIH biosketch format exactly
6. **Budget Errors** - Arithmetic must be perfect
7. **Late Letters of Support** - Request 6+ weeks before deadline
8. **System Failures** - Don't submit at last minute
