### Problem Focus

HFSF targets high uninsured rates and disparities:
- **Broward County:** 15% uninsured
- **Miami-Dade County:** 16.7% uninsured
- **Monroe County:** 18.7% uninsured
- Racial/ethnic disparities among low-income, Black, and Hispanic residents
- Many residents lack usual source of care, leading to costly ER utilization

### 2025-2026 Funding Priorities

1. **Digital health technology** increasing access, patient engagement, and health outcomes
2. **Sustainable, scalable outreach and enrollment models**
3. **Pilot new collaborations** informing enrollment efforts
4. **Expanding alternate access points** (nurse-managed centers, telehealth)
5. **Safety net clinic capacity expansion** with infrastructure support
6. **Advocacy for state and regional policy solutions**

### Recent Initiatives

- **Black Health Summit** - Third annual event focusing on Political Drivers of Health, Black Men's Health
- **Health Equity Goals** - New leadership to advance 2025 objectives
- **$2M in grants** to organizations addressing health equity (2022)
- **$2M investment** in Florida universities for health equity (2024)

### Application Process (4-Step)

1. **Share Your Idea:** Submit Request Inquiry Form via GOapply portal
2. **Co-Development Phase:** Staff collaborate on proposal if aligned
3. **Grant Committee Presentation:** Present to HFSF staff and leadership
4. **Board Review:** Final funding decision by Board of Directors

**Board Meetings:** ~4-5 times annually (March, June, September, December)

## Strategic Recommendations

### Proposal Framing

Lead with **digital health technology** and **SDOH screening**:

> "FIU Herbert Wertheim College of Medicine proposes to expand deployment of the FIU Thrive digital health platform across Miami-Dade County's underserved communities. Thrive's AI-powered SDOH screening and intervention matching capabilities, delivered through the established NeighborhoodHELP household outreach model, directly address HFSF's priority to advance digital health technology that increases access, engagement, and outcomes for uninsured and low-income residents..."

### Key Differentiators to Emphasize

1. **Platform, not program:** Thrive is scalable technology infrastructure
2. **Sustainable model:** NHELP's household relationships persist
3. **Data-informed:** ML-powered, validated screening instruments
4. **Integration:** EHR and community partner connections
5. **Workforce:** Community health worker deployment
