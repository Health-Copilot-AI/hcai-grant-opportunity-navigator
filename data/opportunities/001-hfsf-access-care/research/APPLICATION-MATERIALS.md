## Executive Summary

HFSF uses a four-step application process requiring pre-application contact with staff. Applications are submitted through the GOapply grants portal. The process typically takes 3-4 months from initial contact to board decision.

**Critical Requirement:** Contact program staff before applying (strongly encouraged/expected).

## Application Process (Four Steps)

### Step 1: Share Your Idea

**What to Do:**
1. Create account in GOapply Grants Portal
2. Access Request Inquiry Form under Opportunities tab
3. Complete and submit form

**Portal URL:** https://goapply2.akoyago.com/hfsf

**Form Content:**
- Organization information
- Funding request description
- Alignment with HFSF mission

**Timing:** Concept proposals accepted year-round (some exceptions for specific RFPs)

**Expected Outcome:** Foundation reviews for alignment with mission and priorities

### Step 2: Invited to Submit Proposal

**Trigger:** Request Inquiry deemed aligned with HFSF mission and goals

**Co-Development Activities:**
- Phone calls with program staff
- In-person meetings
- Site visits (in some cases)
- Subject matter expert engagement
- Partnership identification
- Collaborative proposal refinement

**Duration:** Variable (typically 4-8 weeks)

**Outcome:** Invitation to submit full proposal

### Step 3: Grant Committee Presentation

**What Happens:**
- Applicant presents to HFSF staff and leadership
- Committee evaluates proposal

**Timing:** Grant Committee meets approximately 6 weeks before Board meetings

**Possible Outcomes:**
1. **Approval as presented** - proceeds to Board
2. **Approval with recommended changes** - revisions required
3. **Declination** - not advancing

### Step 4: Board Decision

**What Happens:**
- Staff presents approved proposals to Board of Directors
- Board makes final funding decision

**Board Meeting Schedule:**
- March
- June
- September
- December
- (4-5 meetings annually)

## Required Application Documents

### Full Proposal Requirements

| Document | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Proposal Narrative** | Project description, need, approach, outcomes |
| **Budget** | Detailed line-item budget |
| **Budget Narrative** | Justification for budget items |
| **Work Plan** | Timeline and activities |
| **Board of Directors List** | Contact information for board members |
| **501(c)(3) Letter** | IRS determination letter |
| **Form 990** | Most recent tax return |
| **Financial Statements** | Audited or reviewed financials |

### Proposal Narrative Components

Based on HFSF evaluation criteria, narratives should address:

1. **Need Statement**
   - Identified need clearly demonstrated
   - Data supporting the problem
   - Connection to target population

2. **Goals and Objectives**
   - Realistic goals
   - Measurable objectives
   - Clear success indicators

3. **Project Approach**
   - Evidence-based methods
   - Activities and timeline
   - Staffing and roles

4. **Outcomes and Evaluation**
   - Expected outcomes
   - Measurement methods
   - Data collection plan

5. **Collaboration**
   - Community partners identified
   - Leveraged resources
   - Coordinated service delivery

6. **Sustainability**
   - Plans beyond grant period
   - Ongoing funding strategies
   - Institutional commitment

## Budget Guidelines

### Award Parameters

| Parameter | Amount/Range |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Minimum Request** | $20,000 |
| **Maximum Request** | $160,000 (total grant period) |
| **Typical Low** | $50,000 |
| **Typical High** | $120,000 |
| **Duration** | 1-2 years |

### Indirect Cost Policy

- **Rate:** 10-15% of operating budget
- **Documentation:** No itemization required
- **Calculation:** Percentage of direct costs

### Budget Categories (Typical)

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Personnel** | Staff salaries, fringe benefits |
| **Consultants** | Professional services |
| **Supplies** | Program materials, equipment |
| **Travel** | Local travel, mileage |
| **Other Direct Costs** | Varies by project |
| **Indirect Costs** | 10-15% of direct costs |

### Budget Narrative Requirements

- Explain how HFSF funds will be used
- Connect activities to funding requests
- Justify cost reasonableness
- Note matching funds (if any)

## Sample Request Inquiry Content

### Organization Overview
> FIU Herbert Wertheim College of Medicine is a community-based medical school committed to training physician-leaders who serve underserved populations. Our NeighborhoodHELP program, previously supported by HFSF, provides comprehensive health and social services through mobile health centers and household-based care.

### Project Concept
> We seek support to expand deployment of the FIU Thrive digital health platform to strengthen SDOH screening and intervention matching for underserved households across Miami-Dade County. Thrive's AI-powered assessment and referral capabilities will enhance our existing household outreach model, improving access to care and social services for uninsured residents.

### Alignment Statement
> This initiative directly addresses HFSF's Access to Care priorities:
> - **Digital health technology** increasing patient engagement and outcomes
> - **Sustainable outreach models** building on NHELP's 13-year household relationships
> - **SDOH screening** integrating social needs with healthcare delivery
> - **Alternative access points** through mobile health and community-based care

### Request Summary
> We are requesting $[amount] over [duration] to [specific activities]. This project will serve [target population] and achieve [key outcomes].

## Tips for Success

### Do's

1. **Contact staff first** - Required step, not optional
2. **Reference prior funding** - Highlight successful Miami Health ACCESS and NHELP grants
3. **Lead with alignment** - Show clear connection to HFSF priorities
4. **Be specific** - Concrete activities, measurable outcomes
5. **Show collaboration** - Partner involvement strengthens proposals
6. **Plan for sustainability** - Post-grant continuation matters
7. **Use data** - Support need statement with evidence
8. **Request reasonable amounts** - $50,000-$120,000 typical range

### Don'ts

1. **Don't skip pre-contact** - Applications without consultation are disadvantaged
2. **Don't request outside range** - Stay within $20,000-$160,000
3. **Don't ignore geography** - Must serve Broward, Miami-Dade, or Monroe
4. **Don't be vague** - Specific is better than general
5. **Don't overload scope** - Focused projects preferred
6. **Don't ignore matching** - Show additional support if available
