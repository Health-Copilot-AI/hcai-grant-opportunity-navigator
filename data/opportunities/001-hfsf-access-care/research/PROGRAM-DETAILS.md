# Health Foundation of South Florida - Access to Care Program Details

**Research Date:** February 2, 2026
**Program Type:** Rolling Grant Program
**Contact Required:** Yes (before applying)

---

## Executive Summary

The Access to Care program is HFSF's flagship grantmaking initiative focused on increasing access to routine and preventive medical care for children and adults. The program operates on a rolling basis with year-round applications, though applicants must contact program staff before submitting.

**Key Program Facts:**
- Rolling applications accepted year-round
- Grant amounts: $20,000 - $160,000 (typical: $50,000 - $120,000)
- Duration: 1-2 years
- Geographic focus: Broward, Miami-Dade, Monroe Counties
- Staff contact required before applying

---

## Program Context

### The Problem Being Addressed

**South Florida Uninsured Rates (Exceeding National Averages):**

| County | Uninsured Rate | Key Demographics |
|--------|---------------|------------------|
| **Broward** | 15.0% | Low-income workers |
| **Miami-Dade** | 16.7% | Hispanic residents |
| **Monroe** | 18.7% | School-age children |

**Care Access Challenges:**
- Many residents lack a "usual source of care"
- Heavy reliance on emergency rooms (most expensive care setting)
- Low-income, Black, and Hispanic residents face disproportionate barriers
- Financial instability, transportation, housing, food insecurity affect health

**Impact of Unmet Social Needs:**
- Nearly double the depression rate
- 60% higher diabetes prevalence
- 50%+ higher cholesterol and elevated A1c levels
- More than double ED visits
- More than double clinic no-show rates

## Access to Care Strategic Approaches

HFSF pursues four complementary strategies:

### 1. Expanding Health Insurance Coverage
- Outreach and enrollment for KidCare, Medicaid, ACA Marketplace
- Innovative collaborations for sustainable enrollment models
- System-level enrollment infrastructure

### 2. Adopting Alternative Care Delivery Models
- Nurse-managed health centers
- Telehealth/digital health platforms
- Community health workers, peers, and navigators
- Mobile health units

### 3. Growing Safety Net Clinic Capacity
- Operating support for clinics serving uninsured
- Technical and operational infrastructure
- Service maintenance and expansion

### 4. Supporting Healthcare Access Policy Advocacy
- State and regional policy solutions
- Reimbursement advocacy for alternative models
- Financing mechanism reform

## Specific Funding Priorities

### Priority 1: Enrollment and Outreach

**Focus:** Sustainable, scalable enrollment models at local and system levels

**Supported Activities:**
- Health insurance enrollment assistance (KidCare, Medicaid, ACA)
- Education about available coverage options
- Community outreach and navigation services
- Collaborative enrollment initiatives

**Examples:**
- Hispanic Unity's Te Ayudo Program
- Health Council of South Florida initiatives

### Priority 2: Digital Health Technology

**Focus:** Technology-driven solutions increasing access, patient engagement, and health outcomes

**Supported Activities:**
- Digital health platforms for patient engagement
- Telehealth expansion
- Technology enabling remote care access
- AI-powered screening and intervention tools

**Alignment with FIU Thrive:**
- SDOH screening capabilities
- Mobile app for patient access
- AI-powered referral matching
- Data-informed intervention design

### Priority 3: Alternative Access Points

**Focus:** Alternative delivery models that increase access for uninsured and underinsured

**Supported Models:**
- Nurse-managed health centers
- Telehealth services
- Community health worker programs
- Mobile health clinics
- Peer navigators

**Alignment with NeighborhoodHELP:**
- Mobile Health Center infrastructure
- Community-based care delivery
- Household outreach model
- Interdisciplinary student teams

### Priority 4: Safety Net Expansion

**Focus:** Infrastructure support addressing operational and technical barriers at safety net clinics

**Supported Activities:**
- Operating support for existing clinics
- Capacity expansion
- Technical infrastructure upgrades
- Operational improvement initiatives

### Priority 5: Policy Advocacy

**Focus:** State and regional solutions for healthcare access and utilization

**Supported Activities:**
- Policy research and analysis
- Advocacy campaigns
- Coalition building
- Financing mechanism reform efforts

## Application Process

### Four-Step Process

#### Step 1: Share Your Idea

**What:** Submit a Request Inquiry Form through the GOapply Grants Portal

**Portal:** https://goapply2.akoyago.com/hfsf

**Requirements:**
- Create account in grants portal
- Complete Request Inquiry Form
- Describe organization and funding request

**Timing:** Concept proposals accepted year-round (with some exceptions for specific RFPs)

#### Step 2: Invited to Submit Proposal

**What:** If aligned with mission, advance to co-development with program staff

**Activities Include:**
- Phone calls and in-person meetings
- Site visits (in some cases)
- Subject matter expert engagement
- Partnership identification
- Collaborative proposal refinement

**Required Documents (Final Proposal):**
- Proposal narrative
- Budget and budget narrative
- Work plan
- Board of Directors contact list
- IRS 501(c)(3) determination letter
- Form 990
- Financial statements

#### Step 3: Grant Committee Presentation

**What:** Present to HFSF staff and leadership

**Possible Outcomes:**
1. Approval as presented
2. Approval with recommended changes
3. Declination

**Timing:** Grant Committee meets approximately 6 weeks before Board meetings

#### Step 4: Board Decision

**What:** Staff presents approved proposals to Board of Directors

**Timing:** Board convenes 4-5 times annually
- Typical months: March, June, September, December

**Outcome:** Final funding decision

## Evaluation Criteria

Based on HFSF documentation, proposals are evaluated on:

### Primary Criteria

1. **Need Demonstration**
   - Clear identification of community need
   - Data supporting the problem statement
   - Connection to target population

2. **Goals and Outcomes**
   - Realistic objectives
   - Measurable outcomes
   - Clear success indicators

3. **Evidence Base**
   - Research-supported approach
   - Proven or promising practices
   - Learning from prior work

4. **Budget Quality**
   - Clear explanation of fund use
   - Reasonable cost allocation
   - Connection between activities and funding

5. **Collaboration**
   - Community partner involvement
   - Leveraged resources
   - Coordinated service delivery

6. **Sustainability**
   - Plans beyond grant period
   - Ongoing funding strategies
   - Institutional commitment

### Additional Factors

- Alignment with HFSF mission and current priorities
- Organizational capacity and track record
- Prior relationship with HFSF (if applicable)
- Potential for system-level impact
- Innovation and scalability
