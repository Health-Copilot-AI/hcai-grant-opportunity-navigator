## Executive Summary

The Health Foundation of South Florida (HFSF) is the region's largest philanthropic organization dedicated to community health, with over 30 years of service and more than $151 million in cumulative grants distributed since inception. The foundation operates as a health conversion foundation focused on advancing health equity in Broward, Miami-Dade, and Monroe Counties.

**Key Opportunity Indicators:**
- Prior funded relationship with FIU (NeighborhoodHELP Mobile Health Center: $110,000)
- Rolling grant applications accepted year-round
- Strong alignment with digital health, SDOH screening, and community-based care
- Regional focus matches NeighborhoodHELP service area exactly

## Strategic Focus Areas

HFSF organizes its grantmaking around four primary focus areas:

### 1. Access to Care

**Objective:** Increase access to routine and preventive medical care for children and adults

**Funding Priorities:**
- Outreach, education, and enrollment for KidCare, Medicaid, and ACA Marketplace
- Digital health technology increasing patient engagement and outcomes
- Alternative delivery models (nurse-managed centers, telehealth)
- Operating support for safety net clinics
- Policy advocacy for healthcare access

**Strategic Approaches:**
- Expanding health insurance coverage
- Adopting alternative care delivery models
- Growing safety net clinic capacity
- Supporting healthcare access policy advocacy

### 2. Health Systems & Social Needs

**Objective:** Advance a collaborative system that integrates social needs with healthcare delivery

**Key Statistics (2022 Physicians Foundation Survey):**
- 95% of doctors report patients have at least one SDOH affecting health
- Unmet social needs correlate with:
  - Nearly double the depression rate
  - 60% higher diabetes prevalence
  - Over 50% higher cholesterol levels
  - More than double ED visits
  - More than double clinic no-show rates

**Strategic Initiatives:**
1. Business Case Development - Demonstrating ROI for SDOH investments
2. Enabling Infrastructure - Medical-legal partnerships, food pharmacies
3. Technology Platforms - Referral systems connecting health and social services
4. Payment Reform - Advocating for financing mechanisms supporting SDOH

**Key Contact:** Janisse Rosario Schoepp, Chief Strategy Officer (jschoepp@hfsf.org)

### 3. Thriving Community

**Objective:** Foster conditions for physical, mental, and emotional well-being through community development

**Context:**
- 47.6% of Miami-Dade households spend >30% of income on housing
- 42.2% of Broward households spend >30% of income on housing

**Housing Focus:**
- Understanding housing-health connections
- Community advocacy capacity for housing policy
- Integrated health and housing models with supportive services
- Policy initiatives protecting affordable housing resources

**Anchor Alliance Initiative:**
- South Florida Anchor Alliance (SFAA) - coalition of 20+ regional institutions
- Focus areas: Supplier diversity and workforce development
- Launched 2019 with Health Foundation as backbone organization
- 2024: $3 million+ in active grants and program support

### 4. Racial Equity

**Key Programs:**
- Annual Black Health Summit
- Inspiring Women of Health Awards
- Take Your Loved One to the Doctor Campaign
- Community conversations and barbershop talks

## Historical Context

### Origin Story

The Health Foundation of South Florida emerged from a December 2017 convening organized by:
- Health Foundation of South Florida
- Citi Community Development
- Federal Reserve of Atlanta

This gathering brought together anchor institutions to leverage collective spending power for community economic benefit.

## Decision-Making Process

### Board Governance

**Board Meeting Schedule:**
- 4-5 meetings annually
- Typical months: March, June, September, December

**Grant Committee:**
- Meets approximately 6 weeks before Board meetings
- Three possible outcomes: approval as presented, approval with modifications, declination

**Board Composition:**
- 100% independent members
- 13+ independent board members
- Audit and Oversight Committee in place
- Documented conflict of interest, whistleblower, and document retention policies

### Application Timeline

1. **Concept Proposal:** Submit Request Inquiry Form (accepted year-round)
2. **Co-Development Phase:** If aligned, work with program staff
   - Phone calls and in-person meetings
   - Site visits (in some cases)
   - Subject matter expert engagement
   - Partnership identification
3. **Grant Committee Presentation:** Present to HFSF staff and leadership
4. **Board Decision:** Final funding decision

### Critical Success Factors

From FAQs and program documentation:
- **Alignment with mission and values**
- **Clear, measurable goals and outcomes**
- **Evidence-based approaches**
- **Detailed budget explanations**
- **Support from additional funding sources**
- **Sustainable community impact beyond the grant**

## Grantmaking Philosophy

### What They Fund

- Projects exclusively serving Broward, Miami-Dade, and/or Monroe Counties
- Tax-exempt nonprofits (501(c)(3)) or government agencies
- Programs aligned with four focus areas
- Evidence-based and measurable approaches
- Collaborative and sustainable initiatives

### What They Do NOT Fund

Based on general foundation practices and mission alignment:
- Projects outside geographic service area
- Organizations without 501(c)(3) or government status
- Capital construction (unclear, verify with staff)
- Basic/bench research
- Projects lacking community focus

### Indirect Cost Policy

- Supports 10-15% indirect costs
- Calculated as percentage of operating budget
- No itemization required

## Recommended Approach

### Pre-Application Steps

1. **Contact Janisse Rosario Schoepp** (required by HFSF process)
   - Email: jschoepp@hfsf.org
   - Reference prior NeighborhoodHELP funding
   - Express interest in Access to Care program

2. **Prepare to discuss:**
   - FIU Thrive platform capabilities
   - NeighborhoodHELP expansion plans
   - Prior grant outcomes and lessons learned
   - Alignment with HFSF's current strategic priorities

### Proposal Positioning

**Lead with:**
- Digital health technology (Thrive platform)
- SDOH screening capabilities
- Proven household outreach model
- Measurable outcomes from prior HFSF funding

**Emphasize:**
- Platform scalability (technology infrastructure, not single program)
- Sustainability beyond grant period
- Data-informed, ML-powered approach
- Integration with community partners

### Risk Mitigation

1. **Charity Navigator 1-Star Rating:** Address any concerns by emphasizing:
   - Program focus (not administrative overhead)
   - Direct community impact
   - Measurable outcomes

2. **Rolling Timeline:** Plan for 3-4 month process from initial contact to funding decision
