## Mission Statement

> "Every person deserves equitable care and education."

The LabCorp Charitable Foundation was established with a core belief in advancing quality healthcare access for all while supporting education and local communities. The foundation focuses on bringing quality healthcare access to underserved populations and fostering healthier communities.

## Funding Priorities

### Primary Grant Categories

1. **Health and Welfare**
   - Healthcare access for underserved populations
   - Patient services and support programs
   - Medical research and health screenings
   - Healthy lifestyle advocacy

2. **Education**
   - STEM programming initiatives
   - Literacy education
   - Youth development programs

3. **Community**
   - Food pantries and meal programs
   - Community development initiatives
   - Local nonprofit support

### Health Equity Focus Areas

The foundation demonstrates a strong commitment to health equity, specifically targeting:

| Population | Program Examples |
|------------|------------------|
| **People of Color with Diabetes** | American Diabetes Association Patient Co-Pay program for earlier intervention and prevention |

| **Black Men** | Zero - The End of Prostate Cancer ZERO360 patient navigation program |

| **Low-Income Youth** | Center for Science, Technology, and Leadership tutoring and behavioral health services |

### Screening Program Support

The foundation actively supports medical research and screenings promoting healthy lifestyles, including:
- Free mammogram programs for minority communities
- Prostate cancer screening navigation for underserved populations
- Diabetes screening and patient access programs
- Health education outreach in minority communities

## Application Process

### Eligibility Requirements

**Required:**
- 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status from IRS (pending status does NOT qualify)
- U.S.-based organization
- Project alignment with one of three supported areas (health/welfare, education, community)

**Excluded Organizations:**
- Individuals
- Political campaigns or organizations
- Religious organizations (for religious purposes)
- Sports clubs
- Hospital capital campaigns
- Organizations without confirmed 501(c)(3) status

### Application Timeline

| Milestone | Timing |
|-----------|--------|
| **Review Cycles** | March and September (twice annually) |
| **Submission Window** | Year-round |
| **Lead Time Required** | 10-12 weeks before project launch |
| **Decision Notification** | Approximately 10-12 weeks after submission |

### Application Requirements

1. Complete online Contributions Request Form (phone solicitations not accepted)
2. Organization description and mission statement
3. Program/event supporting documentation
4. Program budget
5. Description of fund usage

### Application Portal

- **Primary URL**: https://apply.yourcausegrants.com/apply/programs/84fe6f21-f3af-437f-b9de-b3ba1ff79361
- **Application Form ID**: 1752210/forms/7287

### Acceptance Rate
- Approximately 12% acceptance rate for new applicants

## LabCorp Corporate Context

### Parent Company Programs

LabCorp's broader commitment to health equity includes:

1. **Financial Barrier Reduction**
   - Foundation grants to minimize financial obstacles to care
   - Employee volunteer programs supporting underserved communities

2. **Access & Convenience**
   - 2,200+ patient service centers nationwide
   - Hundreds of collection sites at Walgreens locations
   - At-home testing kits through LabCorp OnDemand
   - Decentralized clinical trial formats

3. **Women's Health Focus**
   - Annual health screenings and fertility testing
   - Pregnancy and sexual health services
   - Partnerships with Ovia Health apps

4. **Healthcare Organization Support**
   - Diagnostic Assistant and Insight Analytics tools
   - Population health and socioeconomic data filtering
   - Care gap closure programs for chronic conditions

### Employee Matching Program

LabCorp matches employee contributions to:
- American Cancer Society
- American Diabetes Association
- American Heart Association
- Red Cross
- National Urban League
- United Way

## Strategic Alignment Assessment

### Alignment with FIU NHELP/Thrive Programs

| Factor | Assessment | Notes |
|--------|------------|-------|
| **Health Equity Priority** | HIGH | Explicit focus on communities of color, underserved populations |
| **Screening Programs** | HIGH | Actively funds cancer screenings, diabetes, heart health in minority communities |
| **Community Health** | HIGH | Supports food access, patient navigation, community wellness |
| **Geographic Fit** | MODERATE | National scope; not Florida-specific but supports all 50 states |
| **Grant Size** | MODERATE | Typical $5K-$20K; large grants ($100K+) less common |
| **Academic Medicine** | LOW-MODERATE | Primary focus on direct service organizations |

### Recommended Approach

1. **Lead with Health Equity Angle**: Emphasize screening programs and health disparities work in communities of color
2. **Highlight Patient Navigation**: ZERO360-type patient navigation programs are clearly valued
3. **Demonstrate Community Impact**: Food access, chronic disease prevention, underserved population reach
4. **Apply March or September Cycle**: Plan 10-12 weeks ahead
5. **Request Moderate Amount**: $10,000-$25,000 range most likely to succeed for new applicants
